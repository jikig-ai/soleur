## Purpose

The processing serves **two cumulative legitimate purposes**:

1. **Operate the Jikigai-administered Soleur LinkedIn Company Page as a marketing / community / case-study distribution channel.** Jikigai SARL is the controller of `https://www.linkedin.com/company/soleur/` (LinkedIn Company Page slug `soleur`). The Page is the primary B2B presence for Soleur and the named-controller surface under which content (blog posts, release notes, case studies, founder narrative) is distributed to professional audiences. Posts are produced by `scripts/content-publisher.sh` (cron) and submitted via the Community Management API on the `w_organization_social` scope. The Page exists to (i) reach Soleur's target audience where they already exist (LinkedIn is the canonical B2B/SaaS distribution channel), (ii) build a follower base for community signal, (iii) carry case studies that demonstrate Soleur's effects on real engineering organisations.

2. **Consume Page Insights metrics to evaluate marketing effectiveness.** LinkedIn Page Insights API surfaces aggregate engagement metrics (impressions, reactions, comments, shares, follower growth) to the Page admin (Jikigai SARL). Soleur consumes these in aggregate to (i) measure marketing effectiveness per post, (ii) inform editorial decisions about which content classes resonate, (iii) provide quarterly traction-evidence inputs to investor / customer conversations. Aggregate consumption only — no individual-follower targeting, no follower-list extraction beyond LinkedIn's own admin UI surfaces, no DM scraping, no private-data API calls.

The K-bis transfer to Microsoft Ireland Operations Ltd that happens once at the appeal-flow step (Phase 5.4 of the parent `2026-05-19-feat-linkedin-api-reapply-jikigai-plan.md` runbook) is a separate processing activity with its own lawful basis (Art. 6(1)(c) — legal obligation flowing from the LinkedIn Platform Terms and Microsoft's KYC-equivalent business-verification process). It is documented at PA15 lawful-basis row entry (i) and is the load-bearing reason PA15 exists in the Article 30 register; this LIA references it solely to delineate scope (the K-bis transfer is **not** the subject of this LIA).

The purpose is **specific, explicit, and legitimate**:

- **Specific**: limited to operating a Jikigai-owned Company Page and consuming the metrics LinkedIn surfaces to its own admin. Not LinkedIn-wide outreach. Not paid-ad targeting (no LinkedIn Ads product activation in scope). Not Sponsored Content / Lead Gen Forms / Sales Navigator (separate products with their own LIA requirements; re-evaluation trigger fires).
- **Explicit**: stated in advance via this LIA + Article 30 PA15 + the parent plan + the brainstorm CLO assessment carried forward from `2026-05-19-linkedin-community-api-reapply-jikigai-brainstorm.md`.

## Necessity

The processing is **necessary** — meaning a less intrusive alternative cannot achieve the same legitimate purpose — for the following reasons:

1. **Alternative A — use only first-party channels (soleur.ai blog + newsletter), no LinkedIn Page**: Rejected. First-party channels reach existing subscribers / known readers but do not surface to LinkedIn's discovery graph — the channel where professional audiences encounter new B2B/SaaS products. Pure first-party publishing forfeits the legitimate marketing purpose at its definitional boundary (reaching the target audience where they exist). The newsletter (Buttondown, PA3 of the Article 30 register) and the blog (GitHub Pages, PA4) are operated additively, not as substitutes.

2. **Alternative B — use a personal LinkedIn profile of the founder instead of a Company Page**: Rejected. A personal profile is a natural-person surface where the data subject (the founder) is also the controller — confusing the controller/data-subject distinction and creating Art. 4(1) ambiguity. A Company Page is the canonical LinkedIn-recognised surface for an entity (Jikigai SARL) to operate as a controller distinct from its natural-person agents. The Company Page also limits cross-controller blast radius: posts and engagement attach to Jikigai-the-entity, not to Jean Deruelle-the-individual.

3. **Alternative C — operate the Company Page but disable Page Insights consumption**: Rejected as a *substitute*. Without Page Insights, Soleur cannot measure marketing effectiveness; the marketing purpose under §Purpose paragraph 1 collapses into "publish and hope." Page Insights consumption is also limited to data LinkedIn already surfaces to the Page admin by default — disabling it on the consumption side does not delete the data on LinkedIn's side; LinkedIn computes and stores Page Insights regardless. The "disable" option is, in effect, "blind self-imposed restriction without privacy gain."

4. **Alternative D — Page Insights but no posting**: Rejected. A Page without posts is an empty controller surface that accumulates inactive-Page signals (LinkedIn's Search / Recommendation systems deprioritise inactive Company Pages, defeating the purpose's discovery component).

5. **Data minimisation within the necessary processing**: Applied.
   - **Posts**: produced from operator-authored content (case studies, release notes, blog excerpts) — no Soleur-user personal data. Content publisher `scripts/content-publisher.sh` reads markdown from `content/` and posts text + optional public URLs only. No image attachments containing personal data of Soleur users. No tagging of LinkedIn members in posts (no `@mention` of LinkedIn members).
   - **Page Insights consumption**: aggregate metrics only. The LinkedIn API surfaces per-follower data only at higher-tier access levels (Sales Navigator, Marketing Developer Platform) which are not requested. The `w_organization_social` scope used here is posting-only; Page Insights aggregate reads use the implicit Page-admin entitlement of the operator session, not an elevated scope.
   - **No follower-list extraction**: Soleur does not extract the LinkedIn-side follower list to Soleur-controlled storage. The follower list remains on LinkedIn's infrastructure under LinkedIn's controller relationship with each follower.
   - **No persistent Soleur-side copy of identifying engagement metadata**: comment bodies, reactor identities, etc. are read transiently for analytics consumption and are not persisted in Soleur's database. The only Soleur-side persistence is the rolling tracker issue #4046 (operational fallback log; references LinkedIn post IDs not personal data).

6. **Retention proportional to purpose**: aligned with PA15 row (f). Tokens rotate at 60 days (vendor-mandated). The rolling tracker is append-only operational log on GitHub Issues, closed (with `--reason completed`) when queued posts are re-published post-approval. No long-term Soleur-side personal-data retention.

**Conclusion**: the processing is necessary for the legitimate purpose; minimisation has been applied at scope, content, and persistence layers; less intrusive alternatives were considered and rejected for the reasons above.

## Balancing

The balancing test weighs the controller's legitimate interest (above) against the rights and freedoms of the data subjects (LinkedIn Page followers and engagers). Per WP29 Opinion 06/2014 §III.3 and EDPB Guidelines 1/2024 §4, the balancing test considers: (i) nature of the interests, (ii) nature of the data, (iii) reasonable expectations of the data subject, (iv) impact on the data subject, (v) safeguards, plus an explicit (vi) Art. 17 carve-out treatment.

### (i) Nature of the controller's interests

- **Legitimate marketing / community / discovery interest** in reaching Soleur's target B2B/SaaS audience on the canonical professional-network surface for that audience.
- **Operational interest** in measuring marketing effectiveness (Page Insights) so editorial decisions are evidence-based rather than guess-based.
- These are recognised legitimate interests under EDPB Guidelines 1/2024 §3.1.2 (direct marketing) and §3.1.4 (analytics necessary to operate a service the controller is contractually entitled to operate).

### (ii) Nature of the data subject's interests

- The data subjects are **LinkedIn members who have voluntarily either followed the Soleur Page or engaged with a Soleur Page post** (reaction, comment, share). They are adult professionals operating under their own LinkedIn account with their own privacy settings under LinkedIn Ireland as their primary controller for the LinkedIn platform.
- They are not vulnerable data subjects.
- Their interest is in not having their LinkedIn-surface engagement aggregated by the Page admin for marketing-analytics purposes that exceed reasonable Page-operation expectations.

### (iii) Nature of the data

- **Follower identifiers**: display name + profile URL slug, voluntarily made public by the follower's choice to follow a public Company Page on a professional network.
- **Engagement metadata**: timestamps + reaction type + (occasionally) comment body if the engager chose to comment publicly. All of this is **public by design** on LinkedIn — visible to any LinkedIn member who views the Soleur Page or the specific post.
- **Aggregate impressions / reach**: counts only, no per-member attribution at the Soleur consumption layer.
- **No special-category data (Art. 9)**. **No criminal-offence data (Art. 10)**. **No financial data**. **No location data**. **No biometric or health data**.
- Risk profile: low — the data is *by design* public-on-LinkedIn; the controller's processing does not change the public/private character of the data, only its aggregation into a Page-admin analytics view that LinkedIn itself provides as a product feature.

### (iv) Reasonable expectations of the data subject

- A LinkedIn member who **follows** a Company Page has a **strong reasonable expectation** that the Page admin will see follower-count metrics including their own contribution to that count. LinkedIn's own product UX surfaces "X followers" to every Page visitor and "you are following this Page" to the follower themselves; the Page admin's view of an aggregate-follower-count is a documented LinkedIn product affordance under LinkedIn's own Page Insights disclosures.
- A LinkedIn member who **engages with a post** (reaction, comment, share) takes an action LinkedIn explicitly labels as public-by-default; the engagement is visible under the post itself to any viewer. The Page admin's aggregate consumption of engagement metrics is the canonical use case Page Insights exists to serve.
- Members who do not wish to be aggregated into a Page admin's analytics view can (a) unfollow the Page, (b) remove their reaction, (c) delete their comment — all under their own LinkedIn account control. LinkedIn is the primary controller of these consent/withdrawal surfaces.
- Operator transparency: `docs/legal/privacy-policy.md` §4.10 (this PR — #4051) discloses the Page-publication processing activity, names LinkedIn Ireland as recipient under Art. 13(1)(e), and references this LIA. `docs/legal/data-protection-disclosure.md` §2.3(p) mirrors the disclosure in structured form.

### (v) Impact on the data subject

- **Negligible.** The data is public-by-design on the source platform; the Page admin's aggregate consumption is a documented LinkedIn product affordance; the data subject controls their own engagement at all times via their own LinkedIn account.
- **No automated decision-making** in the sense of Art. 22 — Page Insights consumption is aggregate analytics, not per-individual decisioning, and Soleur does not feed Page-engagement signals back into any per-individual decisioning system on Soleur infrastructure (no enrichment of Soleur-user profiles from Page-follower data; no targeting of Page followers via separate channels).
- **No profiling** in the EDPB sense — Soleur does not construct individual profiles of Page followers; aggregate-only consumption.

### (vi) Safeguards (TOMs — Art. 32 cross-reference)

Substantial safeguards reduce residual risk to negligible. The safeguards are mirrored from Article 30 PA15 row (g) and are operationally enforced by the parent #4046 PR (#4047) merged 2026-05-19:

- **Doppler-only secret storage** — `LINKEDIN_ORG_ACCESS_TOKEN` lives in Doppler `prd` + GitHub Actions secrets; never committed; rotation operator-driven via `plugins/soleur/skills/community/scripts/linkedin-setup.sh generate-token`.
- **Per-token expiry monitor** — `.github/workflows/scheduled-linkedin-token-check.yml` introspects both `LINKEDIN_ACCESS_TOKEN` and `LINKEDIN_ORG_ACCESS_TOKEN` weekly; opens a per-token `[Action Required]` issue at expiry detection and closes it on next valid check.
- **Vendor-blocked error class routes to rolling tracker** — `classify_linkedin_error` in `scripts/content-publisher.sh` matches missing-token, HTTP 401, HTTP 403, and `w_organization_social` scope errors → `append_to_linkedin_tracker` (idempotent append to #4046).
- **DNS verification record managed in Terraform** — `apps/web-platform/infra/jikigai-com.tf` narrow aliased `cloudflare.jikigai_com` provider backed by a `Zone:DNS:Edit on jikigai.com`-only API token; no `lifecycle.ignore_changes` so dashboard drift is surfaced at next `terraform plan`.
- **`LINKEDIN_ALLOW_POST` flag** — `"true"` in the workflow env is a posting kill-switch; flipping to anything else (or unset) routes posting to the rolling-tracker fallback without surfacing API errors.
- **Aggregate-only Page Insights consumption** — operational policy reinforced by the absence of any code path that requests per-member Insights API surfaces; the lack of code IS the safeguard.
- **No follower-list extraction** — no code path extracts the follower list to Soleur-controlled storage; the follower list remains on LinkedIn under LinkedIn's controller relationship.
- **No DM scraping / no `@mention` posting** — content publisher posts text + public URLs only; no LinkedIn-member mentions, no DM access, no private-data API scope.
- **Privacy Policy + DPD disclosure** — Art. 13(1)(e) transparency satisfied via §4.10 / §2.3(p) and the dual-recipient enumeration (LinkedIn Ireland + Microsoft Ireland) — landed by this PR.
- **Art. 17 carve-out disclosure** — Privacy Policy §8.1 and DPD §10.3 add the carve-out paragraph for LinkedIn-published content: Soleur deletes its source copy and issues a deletion request to LinkedIn, but cannot guarantee removal from LinkedIn's cached or replicated systems. This is per EDPB Guidelines 5/2019 §III.B which acknowledges that controllers' Art. 17 obligations toward third-party-replicated content extend to best-effort cascade requests, not absolute removal.

### Conclusion

The controller's legitimate interest (marketing + operational) **substantially outweighs** the data subjects' minor interest in not having their voluntarily-public LinkedIn engagement aggregated by the Page admin, given:

- The data is public-by-design on the source platform; the Page admin's aggregate consumption is a documented LinkedIn product affordance.
- The data subjects voluntarily took the action being aggregated (follow / react / comment) on a platform whose Pages-as-controller model they implicitly accept by participating.
- The reasonable expectation of Page-admin analytics is overwhelming for any LinkedIn Company Page.
- The impact is negligible and the data subjects retain full control via their own LinkedIn account.
- The safeguards stack is extensive and load-bearing — most importantly the aggregate-only consumption boundary and the absence of any cross-system enrichment.

**Balancing outcome: legitimate interest prevails.** Art. 6(1)(f) is the appropriate lawful basis for operating the Jikigai-administered Soleur LinkedIn Company Page and consuming aggregate Page Insights.

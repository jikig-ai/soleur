# Data Processing Agreement (Template)

This Data Processing Agreement ("**DPA**") template is the customer-facing instrument Jikigai SARL ("**Jikigai**", "**Processor**") executes with a Customer ("**Customer**", "**Controller**") when Jikigai processes Personal Data on the Customer's behalf through the Soleur Web Platform at `app.soleur.ai` ("**Web Platform**").

**Status at this PR (#4330):** **pre-draft / pending trigger.** The DPA is stored under `knowledge-base/legal/` and is NOT served at `docs/legal/data-processing-agreement.md`. It is promoted to `docs/legal/` at the first of three trigger events recorded in `knowledge-base/legal/compliance-posture.md`: (a) first B2B prospect asks "Do you have a DPA?"; (b) first paying Customer organization invites Co-Members under `FLAG_TEAM_WORKSPACE_INVITE`; (c) first EU Customer requests Standard Contractual Clauses for non-EU sub-processors. See `## Recitals` and `## §13 Term & Termination` for the trigger framing.

This template adapts the structure of the publicly published [Vercel DPA](https://vercel.com/legal/dpa), the [Linear DPA](https://linear.app/dpa), and the in-vendor-stack [Buttondown DPA](https://buttondown.com/legal/dpa) and aligns with the EU GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679), the UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018 (UK), and the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP).

## Recitals

(A) **The engagement.** Customer subscribes to the Web Platform under the Soleur Terms & Conditions ("**ToS**") at `docs/legal/terms-and-conditions.md`. The ToS supersession trigger at §3b.4 promotes this DPA into the contractual envelope when a customer-facing DPA is executed.

(B) **Controllership framing under CJEU C-210/16 *Wirtschaftsakademie*.** For Customer Data (as defined in §1.2 below), the Customer is the controller within the meaning of GDPR Article 4(7) — the Customer determines the **purposes** of processing (the Customer's chosen workspace use case), the **means** of processing (which Co-Members to invite, which scope grants to authorize, which message templates to send, when to flip operational flags), and the **timing** of processing. Jikigai acts as processor within the meaning of GDPR Article 4(8) — Jikigai processes Customer Data only on documented Customer instructions through the Web Platform's user interface and the Soleur ToS. The Soleur ToS Section 3b.1 designates the Workspace Owner as the controller for workspace data; this DPA codifies the bilateral instrument the ToS contemplates. The operator-attested counsel-review for this framing is recorded at `knowledge-base/legal/audits/2026-05-counsel-review-4289.md`.

(C) **Bring-your-own-key ("BYOK") posture for Anthropic.** Jikigai does NOT engage Anthropic PBC as a sub-processor for Customer Data. Anthropic API calls from the Web Platform are made under the Customer's own Anthropic API key, governed by the Customer's bilateral relationship with Anthropic under the Anthropic Commercial Terms §C "authorized users" framework. Anthropic is a Customer-provisioned sub-processor (see §6.3 and Schedule 2's "Customer-provisioned sub-processors (BYOK)" subsection). When the Web Platform ships Soleur-managed (non-BYOK) Anthropic access, this row promotes to the main sub-processor list per the §6.1 30-day notification.

## §1 Subject Matter & Duration

**§1.1 Subject matter.** Jikigai's processing of Personal Data on the Customer's behalf in connection with the Customer's use of the Web Platform.

**§1.2 "Customer Data".** All Personal Data that Customer (or a Co-Member on Customer's behalf) submits to, or generates in, the Web Platform under Customer's Web Platform subscription. Customer Data includes the categories enumerated in Schedule 1.

**§1.3 Duration.** This DPA is co-terminus with the Customer's Web Platform subscription (Soleur ToS §14). GDPR Article 28(3)(g) (deletion-or-return) and Article 28(3)(h) (audit cooperation) obligations survive termination for 12 months as set out in §13 (the 12-month survival preserves Customer's audit-verification right to confirm deletion; it does NOT extend the 30-day deletion deadline in §13.4). Confidentiality and §12 liability carve-outs survive indefinitely as set out in §13.

## §2 Nature & Purpose of Processing

The nature and purpose of processing are described in detail in DPD §2.3 sub-bullets at `docs/legal/data-protection-disclosure.md` and incorporated by reference into this DPA. Summarised here:

- **(a) Draft generation and message storage.** Storage of Customer-authored messages, conversations, and AI-generated drafts under the Customer's subscription.
- **(b) Action-sends audit ledger.** Recording cryptographic signatures of Customer's clicks on Send for low-stakes and brand-critical external sends (`action_sends` table) as Article 5(2) accountability evidence.
- **(c) Scope-grants ledger.** Recording the Customer's per-action-class authorization grants (`scope_grants` table) as the consent substrate for autonomous and one-click action classes.
- **(d) Account, subscription, and authentication.** Storage of email addresses, hashed passwords, authentication tokens, session data, OAuth identity data, encrypted API keys (AES-256-GCM), subscription metadata, and Stripe customer + subscription IDs.
- **(e) Knowledge-base storage.** Storage of Customer-provided knowledge-base files (`kb_files`, `kb_chunks`) and synchronisation from the Customer's connected GitHub repository.

## §8 Personal Data Breach Notification (Articles 33 + 34 GDPR)

**§8.1 Notification window.** Jikigai shall notify Customer of any Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Data **without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours** after Jikigai becoming aware of the breach, by email to Customer's `legal@`-equivalent contact recorded at DPA execution. Customer is responsible for any onward notification to the competent supervisory authority under Article 33 and to data subjects under Article 34.

**§8.2 Notification content.** Jikigai's notification shall include, to the extent known: (a) the nature of the Personal Data Breach including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned and the categories and approximate number of personal-data records concerned; (b) the name and contact details of Jikigai's contact point; (c) the likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach; and (d) the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the Personal Data Breach, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

**§8.3 Detection layer.** Detection is mediated by Sentry (Functional Software GmbH, DE region — Standard Contractual Clauses) and the structured-log + Better Stack pipeline disclosed in DPD §2.3(m). The pseudonymisation boundary (`userIdHash` HMAC-SHA256) is recorded in DPD §2.3(m) and operates at the emission boundary so that re-identification is not possible from the telemetry alone.

**§8.4 Post-incident report ("PIR").** Jikigai operates a redaction-gated PIR template at `knowledge-base/engineering/operations/post-mortems/` for material incidents. Customer may request access to the PIR (redacted) for a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Data; redactions are limited to other customers' data and Jikigai-internal security details.

## §9 Security TOMs (Article 32 GDPR)

The detailed technical and organisational measures are set out in **Schedule 4**. A summary follows:

- **Encryption in transit:** TLS 1.3 minimum on all Customer-facing endpoints (`app.soleur.ai`, `api.soleur.ai`).
- **Encryption at rest:** AES-256-GCM for stored BYOK credentials; Supabase platform encryption for the database; Cloudflare R2 platform encryption for object storage.
- **Pseudonymisation:** `userIdHash` HMAC-SHA256 at the structured-log emission boundary (DPD §2.3(m)); customer-email-hash at the Inngest webhook boundary (DPD §2.3(o)); recipient-id-hash at the action-sends ledger (DPD §2.3(r)/(s)).
- **Access control:** Per-tenant Row Level Security (RLS) on every database table holding Customer Data; the load-bearing `is_workspace_member(workspace_id, auth.uid())` SECURITY DEFINER helper (migration `053_organizations_and_workspace_members`, `search_path = public, pg_temp`) enforces team-workspace boundary.
- **Authentication:** Supabase Auth (magic link + OAuth via Google, Apple, GitHub, Microsoft); Stripe SCA for payment-method changes.
- **Integrity:** WORM (write-once-read-many) triggers on accountability ledgers (`action_sends_no_mutate`, `scope_grants_no_mutate`, `template_authorizations_no_mutate`, `workspace_member_attestations_no_mutate`, `workspace_member_actions` AFTER trigger); Recital 75 anti-tampering posture.
- **Logging and breach detection:** Sentry + Better Stack (see DPD §2.3(m)); Vector VRL transforms (`pii_scrub_*`) at the log-egress boundary.
- **Sub-processor due diligence:** Vendor DPA Status table at `knowledge-base/legal/compliance-posture.md`.
- **Data minimisation:** DSAR allowlist (`apps/web-platform/server/dsar-export-allowlist.ts`); Article 17 erasure cascade in `apps/web-platform/server/account-delete.ts`.

## §10 Audit Rights (Article 28(3)(h) GDPR)

**§10.1 Annual audit right.** Customer may exercise an audit right once per calendar year. Jikigai shall provide reasonable cooperation in response to a written audit request delivered to `legal@jikigai.com` with at least **30 days' prior notice**, identifying the scope of the audit. Customer bears all costs of the audit, except where the audit identifies material non-compliance by Jikigai, in which case Jikigai bears its own costs and reasonable third-party-auditor fees.

**§10.2 Information-provision in lieu of in-person inspection.** Jikigai shall fulfil Customer's audit right by providing, on Customer's written request: (a) the most recent operator-attested compliance posture summary at `knowledge-base/legal/compliance-posture.md`; (b) the most recent counsel-review audits in `knowledge-base/legal/audits/`; (c) the relevant operator-attestation records for the processing activities under audit (e.g., the most recent gdpr-gate report); and (d) such other documentation as Customer reasonably requires.

**§10.3 SOC 2 substitute roadmap.** Jikigai does NOT hold a SOC 2 Type II audit report at the date of this template. Jikigai will evaluate engagement of a SOC 2 Type II auditor on a reasonable-best-efforts basis following execution of the first Customer DPA and will provide Customer with a status update on this evaluation no later than 12 months after first DPA execution. Nothing in this §10.3 constitutes a binding commitment to obtain a SOC 2 Type II report by a date certain; engagement timing depends on Jikigai's resourcing and auditor availability at the relevant time. If and when a SOC 2 Type II report is obtained, it fulfils §10.2 audit cooperation in lieu of operator-attested summaries (Vercel "audit via SOC 2 report" precedent at [vercel.com/legal/dpa](https://vercel.com/legal/dpa) §9). The Vendor DPA Status table at `knowledge-base/legal/compliance-posture.md` tracks the evaluation status.

**§10.4 Anti-audit-fatigue cap.** Customer's right to audit is limited to once per calendar year (§10.1) and to information-provision under §10.2 unless material non-compliance is identified. This adapts Linear DPA §7.4 to prevent customer-side audit-fatigue attacks against Jikigai's operator-attested compliance posture.

**§11.2 Non-EU sub-processors — SCCs Modules 2 + 3.** Where an Authorized Sub-processor processes Customer Data in a third country without an adequacy decision under Article 45 GDPR, the parties incorporate by reference the Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021 ("**SCCs**") in **Module 2 (Controller-to-Processor)** for the Jikigai-to-sub-processor transfer AND **Module 3 (Processor-to-Sub-processor)** for the flow-down. Customer is the data exporter under Module 2; Jikigai is the data importer + data exporter under Module 3; the sub-processor is the data importer under Module 3. Sub-processors covered by §11.2: **Stripe** (US — EU-US DPF + SCCs as belt-and-suspenders), **Resend** (US — DPF + SCCs), **Sentry** (DE-primary processing; SCCs apply to any onward US transfer under Sentry's standard EU-region terms), **Bullet Train Ltd (Flagsmith)** (UK-based controller currently covered by EC adequacy decision (EU) 2021/1772; UK IDTA + EU SCCs Modules 2+3 incorporated as belt-and-suspenders against adequacy review-lapse and to flow down to Flagsmith's own US sub-processors per Schedule 2), **LinkedIn Ireland** (Section 2.3(p) — joint controller, not processor, but SCC Module 1 reference is included for transparency), **Microsoft Ireland** (Section 2.3(p)(iii) — controller-to-controller K-bis transfer; Microsoft EU Data Boundary applies).

**§11.3 DPF reliance.** Where an Authorized Sub-processor is certified under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework ("DPF"), Jikigai relies on DPF as the primary transfer mechanism, with SCCs incorporated as belt-and-suspenders against DPF invalidation risk.

**§11.4 UK customers (deferred).** Where Customer or a Co-Member is subject to UK GDPR, the parties incorporate the UK International Data Transfer Addendum to the SCCs ("**UK IDTA**") as Schedule 5 — Soleur has no UK customer as of the date of this template; UK IDTA is reserved as a "promote on request" annex. **§11.5 Switzerland (deferred).** Where Customer or a Co-Member is subject to the Swiss FADP, the parties apply the Swiss adaptations to the SCCs in Module 4 (Controller-to-Controller corollary). Switzerland-specific terms are reserved as Schedule 5 jurisdiction-specific annex.

### Research insight — SCC Module attribution

The EDPB Guidelines recommend Module 2 (C2P) AND Module 3 (P2P) for sub-processor flow-down. Both modules are named in Schedule 3 to satisfy Article 28(4) end-to-end. A customer-onward transfer to a country with no adequacy decision triggers Module 3 + supplementary measures (encryption-in-transit + access controls); §11.2 names supplementary-measures requirement generically and the specific measures live in Schedule 4 TOM categories 6-7.

## §12 Liability & Indemnification

**§12.1 Aggregate cap.** Each party's aggregate liability under this DPA is capped at the same level as the Customer's aggregate liability cap under the Soleur ToS §11.2 — specifically, **the greater of (a) EUR 100 or (b) the total subscription fees paid by Customer to Jikigai under the Web Platform subscription in the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the liability**.

**§12.2 Carve-outs from the cap.** The §12.1 cap does NOT apply to:

- (a) statutory damages payable to data subjects under GDPR Article 82;
- (b) liability arising from gross negligence or wilful misconduct;
- (c) breach of confidentiality obligations under §5.2;
- (d) breach of sub-processor flow-down obligations under §6.2;
- (e) Customer's indemnification obligations under §4.2.

**§12.3 Sub-processor breach recovery.** Jikigai's liability to Customer for a Personal Data Breach attributable to an Authorized Sub-processor is governed by the §12.1 cap and the §12.2 carve-outs. Jikigai will use reasonable efforts to pursue recovery from the responsible sub-processor under the Jikigai-sub-processor DPA. Where Jikigai's net recovery from the sub-processor (after costs of pursuit) exceeds the amount Jikigai has paid Customer for the same Personal Data Breach, Jikigai shall remit the excess to Customer up to the amount of Customer's actual loss.

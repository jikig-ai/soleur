# Statutory Response Catalog — Operator Inbox Triage

This catalog is the human-readable companion to the code-static statutory rule
registry at `apps/web-platform/lib/email-triage/statutory-rules.ts`
(feat-operator-inbox-delegation, #5103). Each section anchor below is the
target of a `catalogAnchor` value in that registry
(`statutory-response-catalog.md#breach`, `#service-of-process`, `#dsar`,
`#regulator`), and each section quotes the registry's `catalogExcerpt` verbatim
— the registry and this catalog MUST stay consistent: the registry is the
single system-of-record for statutory periods (clock display derives from
`received_at` + `dueRule`; there are no clock columns in the database), and
this catalog is the system-of-record for what to do when the clock starts.

Sections appear in the registry's pinned first-match priority order:
**breach > service-of-process > dsar > regulator**.

A matched item is pinned in the inbox until acknowledged. **Acknowledgment is
workflow state, not legal resolution** — the item keeps its clock after
acknowledge, and the daily cron re-pins acknowledged-but-unresolved statutory
items at T-7d and T-2d before the computed due date.

## Breach

**Rule:** `breach-art33` (statutory class `breach`).

**Obligation.** Notification of a personal data breach to the competent
supervisory authority under GDPR Art. 33, and — where the breach is likely to
result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons —
communication to the affected data subjects under Art. 34.

> Registry excerpt (`catalogExcerpt`, quoted verbatim): "A personal data
> breach must be notified to the competent supervisory authority without undue
> delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours of becoming aware of it (GDPR
> Art. 33)."

**Period.** 72 hours from *awareness* (registry `dueRule`: `hours: 72`,
label "notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours (GDPR Art. 33)").
The triage clock runs from the email's `received_at` as a conservative proxy
for awareness; the actual Art. 33 clock anchors on when the controller became
aware of the breach (see the cc-dispatcher P0 mirror `first_observed_at`
convention in the Article 30 register's Cross-Cutting TOMs).

**First-response checklist.**

1. Read the original mail in the Proton `ops@soleur.ai` mailbox (the Sieve
   keep-copy is the durable original; the triage row deliberately persists no
   body).
2. Determine whether the report concerns Jikigai-controlled processing (our
   platform / our processors) or a third party's breach merely notifying us as
   a data subject or customer — only the former starts our Art. 33 clock.
3. Anchor the awareness timestamp in writing (issue or incident record) —
   Art. 5(2) accountability for the 72-hour computation.
4. Open the incident path (`/soleur:incident` PIR scaffold) and assess
   risk-to-rights to decide between "notify CNIL", "notify CNIL + data
   subjects (Art. 34)", or "document why no notification is required
   (Art. 33(1) unlikely-risk carve-out)".
5. CNIL notification template is held by the CLO role (see Article 30 register
   — Incident response TOM).
6. Acknowledge the inbox item only once the above steps are owned — the T-7d /
   T-2d re-pin is the backstop, not the plan.

## Service-of-process

**Rule:** `service-of-process` (statutory class `service-of-process`).

**Obligation.** Respond to formal legal process (summons, subpoena, writ,
assignation, citation à comparaître) within the deadline the instrument
itself states. There is no single statutory period: the period is set by the
issuing court or authority and by the procedural law of the forum.

> Registry excerpt (`catalogExcerpt`, quoted verbatim): "Service of legal
> process (summons, subpoena, assignation) carries the deadline stated in the
> instrument itself — read the document and calendar that date; the one-month
> default here is only a safety net."

**Period.** Per the instrument (registry `dueRule`:
`calendar-month`, label "verify the instrument's own deadline" — a safety-net
default, NOT the legal deadline; the real deadline may be shorter).

**First-response checklist.**

1. Retrieve the original document from the Proton `ops@soleur.ai` mailbox —
   service documents almost always arrive as attachments, and the triage
   pipeline records attachment *metadata only* (never content).
2. Verify authenticity (issuing court/huissier identity, case number) before
   acting; forwarded mail carries no sender authentication (Sieve forwarding
   strips SPF/DKIM context — recorded in the ingress ADR).
3. Calendar the instrument's own deadline immediately; treat the catalog's
   one-month default as void once the real date is known.
4. Engage external counsel — service of process is outside the operator-
   attestation envelope used for routine compliance artifacts.
5. Preserve evidence: do not archive or purge related items (statutory rows
   are retention-exempt by design — `purge_email_triage_items` only deletes
   rows where `statutory_class IS NULL`).

## DSAR

**Rule:** `dsar-art15` (statutory class `dsar`).

**Obligation.** Respond to a data subject's access (Art. 15) or erasure
(Art. 17) request — and any other Art. 15–22 right exercised by email — under
the Art. 12(3) response discipline: without undue delay and at the latest
within one calendar month of receipt, extendable by two further months for
complex/numerous requests with notice inside the first month.

> Registry excerpt (`catalogExcerpt`, quoted verbatim): "A data subject access
> (or erasure) request must be answered without undue delay and at the latest
> within one calendar month of receipt (GDPR Art. 12(3)); the clock runs from
> the day the request arrived."

**Period.** One calendar month from receipt (registry `dueRule`:
`calendar-month`, label "respond within one calendar month (GDPR Art. 12(3))").
Calendar-month semantics, not +30 days: received Jan 31 → due Feb 28/29
(`computeDueDate` clamps to the target month's last day). The clock anchors on
the WORM `received_at` sourced from the Resend event payload's receive
timestamp — never webhook-processing time.

**First-response checklist.**

1. Read the full request in the Proton `ops@soleur.ai` mailbox.
2. Acknowledge receipt to the requester within 5 business days (house SLA per
   Privacy Policy §14 / GDPR Policy §14).
3. Identify the requester class: **Web Platform account holder** → route to
   the self-serve DSAR export (`/dashboard/settings/privacy`) or the
   service-role DSAR worker; **involuntary inbound sender** (someone whose
   only data we hold is the triage row about their own email) → fulfil from
   `email_triage_items` directly (the table is in `DSAR_TABLE_ALLOWLIST`,
   Art. 15+20, `user_id` owner; sender-keyed requests are fulfilled manually
   via the service role); **accountless ex-member** → runbook
   `knowledge-base/legal/runbooks/dsar-accountless-ex-member.md` (Art. 12(6)
   identity verification).
4. For erasure requests from involuntary senders: deletion runs through the
   GUC-gated RPC path (see the LIA's Art. 17 section); statutory-evidence rows
   may be retained under Art. 17(3)(b) — document the override if invoked.
5. If extension is needed (Art. 12(3) second sentence), send the extension
   notice with reasons BEFORE the first month lapses.
6. Acknowledge the inbox item only after steps 2–3 are owned.

## Regulator

**Rule:** `regulator-contact` (statutory class `regulator`).

**Obligation.** Respond to correspondence from a supervisory authority (CNIL,
ICO, EDPB, any EU/EEA DPA) — information requests, mise en demeure, audit
notices — within the period the authority's letter states. Cooperation with
the supervisory authority is itself an obligation (GDPR Art. 31).

> Registry excerpt (`catalogExcerpt`, quoted verbatim): "Correspondence from a
> supervisory authority (CNIL, ICO, any EU DPA) states its own response period
> — verify the deadline in the letter itself; treat one month as the outer
> default while you confirm it."

**Period.** Per the letter (registry `dueRule`: `calendar-month`, label
"verify the instrument's own deadline" — outer default while the stated
period is confirmed; a mise en demeure may set a materially shorter one).

**First-response checklist.**

1. Retrieve the original letter from the Proton `ops@soleur.ai` mailbox;
   verify the sending authority's identity through an independent channel
   (the regulator sender patterns — `@cnil.fr`, `@ico.org.uk`,
   `@edpb.europa.eu` — are recall aids, not authentication; forwarded-mail
   sender identity is an unauthenticated claim).
2. Calendar the letter's stated deadline; replace the one-month default.
3. Pull the accountability pack: Article 30 register
   (`knowledge-base/legal/article-30-register.md`), compliance posture
   (`knowledge-base/legal/compliance-posture.md`), the relevant LIA(s) and
   DPIA screening memos — these are the documents a DPA asks for first.
4. Engage external counsel for anything beyond a routine information request.
5. Do not archive the item; statutory rows are retained for the
   accountability period (purge carve-out on `statutory_class IS NULL`).

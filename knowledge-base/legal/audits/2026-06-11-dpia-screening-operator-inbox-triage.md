# DPIA Screening Memo — Operator Inbox Triage (PA-27)

**Question screened:** does Article 35(1) GDPR require a full Data Protection
Impact Assessment for Processing Activity 27 (AI-assisted triage of inbound
email to `ops@soleur.ai`; feature `feat-operator-inbox-delegation`, #5103)?

**Conclusion: a full DPIA is NOT required at single-operator scale.** The
processing is recorded in the Article 30 register (PA-27) with an Art. 6(1)(f)
LIA (`2026-06-11-operator-inbox-triage-lia.md`), and this screening memo —
with three **named accepted residual risks** — is the pre-merge gate, mirroring
the PA-22 lightweight-register posture. Re-screening triggers are pinned below.

## 1. Screening against the Art. 35(3) indicators and WP29/EDPB criteria

Assessed against Art. 35(3)(a)–(c) and the WP29 DPIA Guidelines (WP248 rev.01)
nine criteria; CNIL's liste des traitements requiring a DPIA was also checked.

| Criterion | Finding |
|---|---|
| Systematic and extensive **automated evaluation / profiling with legal or similarly significant effects** (Art. 35(3)(a) / Art. 22) | **Not met.** The pipeline classifies *emails*, not people; no decision with legal or similar significant effect is taken about any sender by automated means. Statutory classification only *escalates to a human* with a deadline; the LLM summary is advisory, rendered read-only, and structurally cannot write `statutory_class` (closed `MAIL_CLASS_ALLOWLIST` excludes all statutory classes and `probe`). No agent write/acknowledge tool exists in v1 (spec FR9 boundary). |
| **Large-scale processing of special categories** (Art. 35(3)(b) / Art. 9) | **Not met at this scale.** Special-category content *may arrive unsolicited* on an open inbound address, but the processing is one operator's single operational mailbox at solo-company volume (throttled 60/h, LLM-ceiling 200/day) — not "large scale" under WP243/WP248 factors (number of data subjects, volume, duration, geography). The Art. 9 exposure is handled as residual (a) below, not denied. |
| **Systematic monitoring of a publicly accessible area** (Art. 35(3)(c)) | Not met. |
| WP248: **data concerning vulnerable data subjects / imbalance** | Partially engaged: inbound senders are **involuntary data subjects** (no contract, no account). This is the strongest single factor toward a DPIA and is the reason this memo exists; it is offset by the business-correspondence context, the minimal persisted footprint, and the LIA balancing analysis. One criterion alone does not normally trigger a mandatory DPIA (WP248: "in most cases … two criteria"). |
| WP248: **innovative use of technology** | Partially engaged (LLM summarization). Offset by the read-only/no-tools binding, the deterministic pre-LLM fast-path for everything deadline-bearing, and the structural parse-and-discard contract. |
| WP248: matching/combining datasets; data transferred outside the EU; prevents exercise of rights | Matching: none (no cross-referencing of senders against user records). Transfers: Resend (US) + Anthropic (US) under DPF/SCCs — disclosed, bounded, short-windowed. Rights: an Art. 17 path exists for involuntary senders (LIA §Balancing), with a documented Art. 17(3)(b) carve-out. |

Two WP248 criteria are partially engaged (involuntary subjects; innovative
technology), but at single-operator scale, with no Art. 22 effects, no
large-scale Art. 9 processing, structural data-minimisation at rest, and a
deterministic path for all deadline-bearing mail, the residual risk to rights
and freedoms is **not "high"** in the Art. 35(1) sense. A full DPIA is not
required; this screening + LIA + PA-27 is the proportionate Art. 24/25
record.

## 2. Named accepted residual risks

These are accepted **by name** — not mitigated away on paper. Each has an
owner-visible failure mode and a recovery path.

**(a) Art. 9 content surviving into the persisted summary despite the prompt
instruction.** The summarizer system prompt instructs omission of
special-category personal details (verbatim in
`server/email-triage/summarize.ts`: "OMIT special-category personal details
(health, religious or political beliefs, sexual orientation, trade-union
membership, ethnicity — GDPR Art. 9)"). **A prompt instruction is a claim,
not a mechanism: the LLM can ignore it**, and the WORM one-time-set rule on
`summary` makes a leaked summary immutable through ordinary writes.
Accepted because: exposure is one operator's RLS-scoped inbox; bodies are
never at rest; the correction path is Art. 17 row deletion via the GUC-gated
purge RPC (the same gate that enforces WORM authorises the erasure).
Re-screening trigger: first observed Art. 9 leak into a persisted summary.

**(b) Prompt injection distorting summaries (read-only / no-tools bound).**
Anyone on the internet can mail `ops@` adversarial content. The injection
blast radius is bounded to a **misleading summary**: the summarizer has no
tools, its output class is allowlist-coerced (it cannot forge statutory
status, cannot hide mail as a probe), summaries render as plain text nodes
(no markdown/anchor execution surface), notification deep links derive only
from the server-generated DB uuid, and the statutory fast-path runs before
and independently of the LLM. Accepted because: the residual harm is the
operator being misled about one email's content, and the detail view names
the recovery path — the original retained in the Proton `ops@` mailbox
(Sieve forward-and-keep) plus the `resend_email_id` within Resend's 30-day
window. Re-screening trigger: any write/act authority added to the pipeline
(#4671/#4672 boundary) — that converts this residual from "misleading text"
to "induced action" and forces a full DPIA-grade re-assessment.

**(c) Resend 30-day no-delete window.** Resend Inbound retains received
email content (full body, headers, attachments) for **30 days on all plans
with NO deletion API** (verified 2026-06-10) — Jikigai cannot honor an
erasure request against that copy faster than the window expires (Chapter V
/ Art. 28(3)(g) finding). Accepted because: the window is short, fixed, and
disclosed (Privacy Policy §4.13/§5.9, DPD §2.3(aa)/§4.2, PA-27, Vendor DPA
row); transfer safeguards are in place (EU-US DPF + SCCs); and the
alternative (no managed inbound provider) re-creates the mailbox-credential
architecture rejected on stronger grounds. Re-screening trigger: Resend
ships a deletion API (adopt it) or extends the window (re-balance).

## 3. Why the remaining surface does not change the conclusion

- **Inngest event store (~24h, self-hosted):** subject + sender transit the
  `email/inbound.received` payload by design (metadata-first statutory
  checking); the store is operator-managed and localhost-bound — a PII
  surface (disclosed in PA-27 §(d)), not a recipient.
- **Observability:** `SENSITIVE_KEY_NAMES` gains `subject`/`sender`/`from`/
  `to`; TR3 bans body/sender/subject values from logs, Sentry tags/extras,
  and Error strings — the sub-processor disclosures stay true.
- **Abuse economics:** throttle (60/h) + daily LLM ceiling (200) bound
  attacker-controlled volume; the probe marker is token-authenticated so it
  cannot be used as a mail-suppression channel.

## 4. Re-screening triggers (any one → re-run this screening; (ii) → full DPIA posture)

5. Quarterly review alongside the Article 30 register cadence.

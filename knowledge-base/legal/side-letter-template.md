## Recitals

WHEREAS the Owner operates a Soleur Web Platform workspace (the "Workspace") gated under Jikigai's `FLAG_TEAM_WORKSPACE_INVITE` allowlist, and Jikigai's Terms & Conditions Section 3b designates the Owner as the data controller (within the meaning of Article 4(7) GDPR) of all personal data processed by the Web Platform on behalf of the Workspace;

WHEREAS the Acceptable Use Policy Section 5.5 requires the Owner to attest that every invitee is, at the time of invitation, party to an employment, contractor, or consultancy agreement obligating confidentiality and intellectual-property assignment terms equivalent to this Side Letter;

WHEREAS the Member's access to the Workspace will produce data and outputs that the Member would otherwise be obligated to treat as confidential and to assign to the Owner under the Member's underlying engagement;

NOW THEREFORE the Parties agree as follows.

## §1. Confidentiality

1.1 The Member shall treat as Confidential Information all non-public data, code, prompts, knowledge-base content, model outputs, conversation history, customer or counterparty identifiers, and operational metadata that the Member accesses, observes, or generates through the Workspace.

1.2 The Member shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as required by the Member's lawful duties under the underlying employment, contractor, or consultancy agreement with the Owner.

1.3 The Member shall not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing the Member's lawful duties under the underlying agreement with the Owner.

1.4 The confidentiality obligations in §1.1 through §1.3 survive termination of this Side Letter and of the underlying agreement for a period of five (5) years.

## §2. Intellectual Property Assignment

2.1 All work product produced by the Member through the Workspace — including code, documentation, designs, knowledge-base entries, prompts, model outputs, and derivative works thereof — shall vest in the Owner as work made for hire (or, where work-for-hire doctrine does not apply, the Member hereby irrevocably assigns to the Owner all right, title, and interest in such work product).

2.2 The Member retains all rights in the Member's pre-existing intellectual property (the "Member Background IP") that the Member identifies in writing to the Owner before introducing it into the Workspace. To the extent Member Background IP is incorporated into work product, the Member grants the Owner a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, sub-licensable license to use, modify, and distribute the Member Background IP as embedded in the Owner's work product.

2.3 The Member shall execute, at the Owner's reasonable request, any further documents necessary to perfect the Owner's title in the work product (including patent, copyright, and trademark assignments).

## §3. Workspace-Activity-Logged Acknowledgement

3.1 The Member acknowledges and agrees that the Owner has full read access, through the Soleur Web Platform's user interface and underlying audit logs, to all activity the Member performs in the Workspace, including (without limitation): conversations the Member participates in; knowledge-base queries the Member submits; BYOK API-key usage; per-action-class scope grants and revocations; per-send signature ledger records ("`action_sends`"), including recipient identifier hashes ("`recipient_id_hash`"), per-send body hashes ("`per_send_body_sha256`"), and template hashes ("`template_hash`"); template-authorization records ("`template_authorizations`"); and, where the Web Platform records cross-member action provenance through the `workspace_member_actions` audit ledger, every row attributable to the Member.

3.2 The Member further acknowledges that the Owner may export, retain, and disclose these audit records as necessary for accounting, compliance, dispute resolution, and Article 5(2) GDPR accountability obligations, and that no expectation of privacy attaches to the Member's in-Workspace activity vis-à-vis the Owner.

## §4. Audit-Log Cross-Member Visibility

4.1 The Member acknowledges that other Co-Members of the same Workspace are recipients (within the meaning of Article 13(1)(e) GDPR) of the Member's in-Workspace metadata for operations in which they participate, including the records enumerated in §3.1.

4.2 The Member shall not exfiltrate, retain outside the Workspace, or onward-disclose any other Co-Member's audit records — including `recipient_id_hash`, `per_send_body_sha256`, or `template_hash` values — outside the lawful scope of the Member's duties under the underlying engagement with the Owner. Any such exfiltration or onward disclosure is a material breach of this Side Letter and a violation of Jikigai's Acceptable Use Policy.

4.3 The Member's independent rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection (Articles 15 through 22 GDPR) against Jikigai over rows identifiable to the Member personally are not modified by this Side Letter.

## §5. Term, Termination, and Post-Termination

5.1 This Side Letter takes effect on the date both Parties sign and remains in force for the duration of the underlying engagement between the Owner and the Member, plus the survival periods stated herein.

5.2 Either Party may terminate this Side Letter by written notice to the other; termination of the underlying engagement automatically terminates this Side Letter.

5.3 Upon termination, the Owner shall promptly remove the Member from the Workspace through the Soleur Web Platform invite-management interface, and the Member shall: (a) cease all access to the Workspace; (b) return or destroy (at the Owner's election) all Confidential Information in the Member's possession; and (c) execute any documents reasonably required to perfect the Owner's title in any outstanding work product per §2.

5.4 The confidentiality obligations of §1, the intellectual-property assignments of §2, and the acknowledgements of §3 and §4 survive termination.

## §6. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

6.1 This Side Letter is governed by the laws of France, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

6.2 Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Side Letter shall be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Paris, France, subject to any mandatory consumer-protection rules that may apply to the Member as a natural person.

## §7. Customer-DPA Supersession

7.1 The Parties acknowledge that this Side Letter is a bilateral instrument between the Owner and the Member; Jikigai is not a party. The Side Letter requirement may be superseded in whole or in part by a customer-facing Data Processing Agreement published by Jikigai under Terms & Conditions Section 3b.4. Upon such supersession, the obligations of this Side Letter that are inconsistent with the superseding instrument shall be deemed amended to conform; the obligations that are not addressed by the superseding instrument remain in force.

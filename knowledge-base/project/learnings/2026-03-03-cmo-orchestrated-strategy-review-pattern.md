# Learning: CMO-Orchestrated Strategy Review Pattern

## Problem

Soleur had fragmented marketing artifacts across 8+ files (brand guide, content plan, content audit, AEO audit, competitive intelligence, business validation, multiple brainstorms) but no unified marketing strategy document. A 15-piece content plan from Feb 19 was 100% unexecuted for two weeks. Seven cascade documents (content strategy, pricing strategy, SEO refresh queue, 4 battlecards) were generated during competitive analysis but never committed. The root cause was a mix of capacity constraints and market shift (Anthropic Cowork Plugins changed the competitive landscape).

## Solution

Deploy the CMO domain leader to orchestrate a unified strategy review in four stages:

1. **Assess** — Read all existing artifacts, identify what's current vs stale, diagnose why execution stalled (capacity + market shift, not strategy quality)
2. **Recommend** — Produce unified positioning informed by validated moats (compounding knowledge base, cross-domain coherence, workflow orchestration depth) and realistic capacity constraints
3. **Delegate** — Specialists generate cascade documents (content strategy, pricing strategy, SEO refresh queue, battlecards) aligned to the unified narrative
4. **Integrate** — All documents linked via YAML dependencies with review cadence to prevent future staleness

Output: 8 documents (1 unified strategy + 3 cascade docs + 4 battlecards), all coherent and cross-referenced.

## Key Insight

When a strategy exists but execution stalled, the answer is usually not "new strategy" but "unified strategy that accounts for WHY execution stalled." The CMO orchestration pattern works because it parallelizes specialist work while maintaining narrative coherence. The critical design decision is making capacity constraints explicit line items in the execution plan — the Feb 19 plan failed precisely because it assumed unlimited bandwidth.

A second insight: cascade documents (content strategy, pricing strategy, battlecards) generated by specialist agents during competitive analysis runs are ephemeral unless explicitly committed. Treat cascade document generation as a two-step process: generate, then verify committed. CI-generated artifacts that aren't committed are effectively lost.

# Sentry Residency Cleanup — Brainstorm

## Context

Issue #3861 surfaced a contradiction between Article 30 register PA8 (DE region, Functional Software GmbH, intra-EU) and the AC14 migration-audit artifact at `knowledge-base/legal/audits/sentry-migration-audit-2026-05-15.md` (`API host: sentry.io` + 8 cron monitors). The issue body framed three remediation tracks (US canonical / DE canonical with cluster surgery / dual-region) and recommended a live probe to disambiguate.

The probe ran. The picture narrowed sharply from the issue's worst-case framing.

## User-Brand Impact

- **Artifact:** Article 30 register PA8 §5(2) accountability evidence — `knowledge-base/legal/audits/sentry-migration-audit-<YYYY-MM-DD>.md`, regenerated by `reusable-release.yml` at each release.
- **Vector 1 (refined out by probe):** Initially framed as unauthorized international transfer of PII to a non-EEA cluster. **Probe disproves:** all user-event ingest flows to DE per the prod DSN; no personal data was transferred to US.
- **Vector 2 (load-bearing):** Misleading §5(2) accountability evidence. A CNIL inspector reading `API host: sentry.io` in the artifact named by PA8's last line would conclude the platform is US-resident, contradicting the register's factual DE claim. Each release regenerates the wrong-shape evidence.
- **Vector 3 (operational):** IaC tfstate at `apps/web-platform/infra/sentry/` is pinned against the US shadow org. Next `apply-sentry-infra` run continues to produce orphaned resources on the wrong cluster.
- **Threshold:** Single-user incident. The register's residency claim is the load-bearing factual representation made to data subjects (Privacy Policy §5.10, DPD §2.3(m), GDPR Policy operational-telemetry bullet). Its supporting accountability evidence must not contradict it.

13. Optional: config-driven residency guard in `apps/web-platform/sentry.{client,server}.config.ts`, gated on `SENTRY_RESIDENCY_EXPECTED=de` env — log to stderr + skip init on DSN cluster mismatch; do **not** throw (would break boot on legitimate future migration). Include only if total LOC < 20; otherwise file as a separate follow-up.

We explicitly **do not**:

- Modify the public-facing legal corpus (`docs/legal/privacy-policy.md`, `data-protection-disclosure.md`, `gdpr-policy.md`, or their Eleventy mirrors). The DE residency claims there are already factually correct.
- Modify ADR-031 prose. The "Phase 0.1.5 validated DE region support" comment validated provider *capability*, not the production org's residency — the residency claim itself is correct; only its supporting evidence and IaC alignment were wrong.
- Add a generalized residency-vs-runtime check to `gdpr-gate`. YAGNI: one concrete instance.
- File this as a notifiable breach. Article 33 does not trigger.
- Engage counsel review. CLO downgraded the counsel trigger after the probe disproved the issue body's "data is currently in-flight on a US plane" framing.

## Why This Approach

- **Probe-driven reframing.** The issue body's worst-case framing (Track 1 vs Track 2 vs Track 3) collapsed to Track 2 once the DSN cluster substring was checked. Reading `o4511123328466944.ingest.de.sentry.io` from Doppler `prd` is faster than spawning leaders against three speculative tracks. (Captured as a learning: probe before triad spawn when the issue itself names a mechanical disambiguator.)
- **Public legal corpus stays.** CLO's read: DE residency claim is factually correct; touching the public corpus adds review surface and risks introducing inconsistency. Only the supporting accountability evidence + the IaC config of record need correction.
- **Decoupled phases neutralize billing-gate latency.** CPO's half-step: A1 is zero-cost and stops wrong-shape evidence regeneration on the next release. A2 waits on operator payment-method entry without holding A1 hostage.
- **State-rm before API delete is mandatory.** CTO sharp edge: reverse order means terraform sees drift on next plan and could re-create on US. Workflow default flip must also land BEFORE state surgery — otherwise step 11's apply re-binds to US via stale default.
- **Config-driven residency guard, not hard-throw.** CTO sharp edge: throwing on DSN mismatch breaks boot on legitimate future migrations. Gating on `SENTRY_RESIDENCY_EXPECTED=de` env makes the assertion explicit + override-able.

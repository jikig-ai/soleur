## Talk Tracks

### "I already use Cursor. Why do I need Soleur?"

**Response:** "You do not need to choose. Cursor is an IDE for writing code. Soleur is an AI organization for running a company. They operate at different layers. Cursor makes you a faster engineer. Soleur connects your engineering work to the rest of your business -- your brand guide shapes how you name things, your competitive analysis informs what you build next, your legal review catches compliance issues before they ship. If you are a solo founder running a real company, the engineering is only 30% of the work. Soleur handles the other 70%."

### "Cursor's Bugbot already does code review."

**Response:** "Bugbot catches general issues and auto-fixes what it can -- 35% merge rate is impressive for automated fixes. Soleur's review is different in two ways: first, it runs 13+ specialized reviewers (security, performance, architecture, accessibility, test coverage, and more) that catch different classes of issues. Second, the review has business context. Soleur's security reviewer knows about your privacy policy. The architecture reviewer knows about your infrastructure decisions from 3 months ago. That contextual depth is the difference between a linter and a CTO review."

### "Cursor has $1B ARR and $29.3B valuation. How can you compete?"

**Response:** "Soleur does not compete with Cursor. Cursor owns the IDE layer for engineering. Soleur provides the business operations layer that Cursor does not and will not build. Cursor's entire business model is built on engineering productivity. They have no incentive to build marketing agents, legal review, or operational workflows. Soleur fills the gap between 'I can code faster' and 'I can run a company.' The relationship is complementary, not competitive."

### "Won't Cursor add business features eventually?"

**Response:** "Cursor raised $29.3B on being the best AI coding tool. Their product roadmap is cloud agents, better code editing, more language models, and developer experience. Adding marketing, legal, and operations agents would dilute their focus and confuse their market positioning. IDE companies stay in the IDE. Soleur's bet is that the business operations layer is a separate product, not a feature of a code editor."

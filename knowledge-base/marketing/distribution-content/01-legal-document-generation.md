## IndieHackers

**Title:** I generated 9 legal documents for my SaaS in days instead of months. Here's what happened.

**Body:**

I'm a solo technical founder building Soleur, a Company-as-a-Service platform. Like every founder, I needed legal documents before launch -- Terms & Conditions, Privacy Policy, GDPR Policy, Cookie Policy, CLAs, the whole suite. Nine documents total.

I got a reality check fast. A technology lawyer in France or the US charges EUR 300-500/hour for SaaS legal document drafting. A full compliance suite with dual-jurisdiction coverage (my company is French, users are global) runs EUR 9,000-25,000 and takes 3-6 weeks. Even a startup package from a boutique firm starts around EUR 5,000 for a basic set -- without CLAs or regulatory benchmarking.

I didn't have EUR 9,000 or 6 weeks. So I built two AI agents: a legal-document-generator and a legal-compliance-auditor.

**How it worked:**

The generator produced first drafts from company context -- entity name, address, product architecture, data practices. The auditor then ran regulatory benchmarking against GDPR Articles 13/14, CCPA requirements, ICO cookie guidance, and CNIL recommendations. Multiple rounds of iteration.

The system caught a real mistake: governing law was set to Delaware (inherited from US templates). The auditor flagged it and corrected it to French law / Paris courts across all 9 documents consistently. That's the kind of cross-document coherence that breaks down when you use separate templates.

**The result:**

9 legal documents totaling 17,761 words across 1,872 lines. Each one addresses dual-jurisdiction concerns, references correct data architecture (local-first, no server-side data collection), and cross-references the others. All deployed live.

**What compounded:**

The legal-compliance-auditor is now a reusable capability. The CLO domain leader auto-joins brainstorm sessions when legal implications come up. Future product decisions get legal assessment without a separate workflow. The investment in building these agents pays forward on every subsequent legal question.

The cost comparison isn't the point -- it's the capability that persists. A lawyer gives you documents. This approach gives you a legal department.

Full case study with the complete breakdown: https://soleur.ai/blog/case-study-legal-document-generation/

**Title:** How I handled legal compliance as a solo founder without spending EUR 9,000+

**Body:**

I needed 9 legal documents for my SaaS -- Terms & Conditions, Privacy Policy, GDPR Policy, Cookie Policy, Acceptable Use Policy, Data Protection Disclosure, Disclaimer, and two Contributor License Agreements. Dual-jurisdiction (French company, global users including EU and US).

Got quotes from lawyers: EUR 9,000-25,000 for the full suite, 3-6 weeks timeline. A startup legal package starts around EUR 5,000 but wouldn't cover CLAs or the regulatory benchmarking I needed for GDPR compliance.

I ended up building AI agents to handle it. Two specialized agents: one generates documents from company context (entity details, product architecture, data practices), and one audits them against regulatory checklists (GDPR Articles 13/14, CCPA, ICO, CNIL).

The interesting part was what the audit agent caught. Governing law was set to Delaware -- a default from US templates that would have been wrong for a French-incorporated company. It flagged and corrected it across all 9 documents. That kind of cross-document consistency is what you lose when you stitch together templates from different sources.

End result: 9 documents, 17,761 words, all cross-referenced, all deployed. Not a replacement for a lawyer's review before serious fundraising, but a solid foundation that would have cost 5 figures to produce from scratch.

The part I didn't expect: the agents are reusable. Any future legal question routes through the same system. It's not a one-time generation -- it's a persistent capability.

## IndieHackers

**Title:** Devin vs. Soleur: Why I use both (and what each one actually covers)

**Body:**

I've been asked a lot whether Soleur competes with Devin. Short answer: no. Longer answer: here's what each platform is actually built to do.

Devin (Cognition) is the best autonomous coding agent I've tested. $20/month. It writes code, opens PRs, runs tests, and as of March 2026 it runs on a schedule with cross-session state. If your bottleneck is engineering throughput -- you have a clear backlog and need implementation capacity -- Devin is a strong solution.

But most solo founders I talk to are not bottlenecked by engineering alone. They're bottlenecked by the cognitive overhead of switching between marketing, legal, finance, ops, product, sales, and support -- in addition to engineering. Devin eliminates one of nine jobs. The other eight are still yours.

That's the gap Soleur addresses. 63 agents, 9 departments, sharing a compounding knowledge base. Every session makes the system smarter about your specific company. The legal agent knows your compliance posture. The marketing agents know your brand guide. The engineering agents know your architecture constraints. One knowledge base, all nine departments, founder remains the decision-maker.

My current stack: Devin for autonomous engineering implementation (close issues, run tests, open PRs). Soleur for organizational intelligence (brand, legal, competitive analysis, financial modeling, ops). They complement each other rather than compete.

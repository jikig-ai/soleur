## Reddit

**Title:** Comparing Devin and Soleur: the autonomous coding agent vs. the AI organization approach

**Body:**

Devin (Cognition AI) recently shipped Scheduled Devins -- recurring autonomous sessions with cross-run state -- and hit a $10.2B valuation. It's arguably the best autonomous software engineer available at $20/month.

I spent time mapping out how it compares to Soleur, which takes a different approach: 63 agents across 9 business domains (engineering, marketing, legal, finance, operations, product, sales, support, growth) with a shared compounding knowledge base.

Key differences I found:

**Depth vs. breadth:** Devin is vertically deep in engineering. Soleur is horizontally broad across the business.

**State model:** Devin's Scheduled Devins carry engineering-task state between runs. Soleur's knowledge base is cross-domain -- brand guide informs marketing, competitive analysis informs sales, legal audit informs engineering. Different scopes of what "persists."

**Workflow:** Devin is task-completion (issue in → PR out). Soleur is lifecycle-based (brainstorm → plan → implement → review → compound). Different philosophies on how much the founder stays in the loop.

**Pricing:** Devin $20/month. Soleur open-source and free.

For founders where engineering is the primary bottleneck and the rest is handled, Devin is strong value. For founders doing all 8 functions solo, the scope mismatch matters.

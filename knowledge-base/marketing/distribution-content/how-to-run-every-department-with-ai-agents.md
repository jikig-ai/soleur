## IndieHackers

**Title:** How I structured AI agent delegation across every business department (playbook)

**Body:**

One of the most useful frameworks I've found for solo building: split every business function into three categories.

Category one: AI executes fully with review. Research, first drafts, competitive monitoring, code review, test writing, infrastructure provisioning.

Category two: AI executes, I review before finalizing. Legal documents, financial models, outbound messaging, anything that commits the company externally.

Category three: I own, AI supports. Strategic positioning, customer relationships, high-stakes negotiations.

The goal is to push category one as far as possible. The hard part is that most AI tools are stateless — each session starts from zero. The marketing agent doesn't know what the legal agent decided. You end up being the relay.

The unlock is a shared knowledge base. When every agent reads and writes to the same compounding context, cross-department coherence emerges: the legal compliance decision reaches marketing before the campaign launches; the product spec change reaches engineering before code is written.

Month one looks like fast tools. Month six looks like an organization.

## LinkedIn Personal

Most AI tools solve the wrong problem for solo founders.

The bottleneck isn't task speed — it's context. The marketing tool doesn't know what the legal tool decided. The engineering agent doesn't know the product spec changed. You end up serving as the relay: reading outputs, summarizing them, pasting context from one tool into the next.

I spent months building an AI organization that shares context across all eight business functions. What I found: the framework that matters most is the delegation split.

Three categories:

Category one: AI executes fully, I review output. Research, first drafts, competitive monitoring, code review, infrastructure provisioning. The AI produces; I verify before it leaves the system.

Category two: AI executes, I approve before finalizing. Legal documents, financial models, outbound messaging, anything that commits the company externally.

Category three: I own, AI supports. Strategic positioning, genuine customer insight, high-stakes judgment calls.

The goal is to push category one as far as possible. The shift from month one to month six is real: early on, you're getting faster at individual tasks. By month six, you have an organization — agents that know your brand guide, reference your legal constraints, and route competitive intelligence to the departments that need it.

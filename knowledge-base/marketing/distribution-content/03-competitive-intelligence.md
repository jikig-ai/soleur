## Discord

The market moves on a weekly cadence. In a single 48-hour window, Anthropic launched engineering plugins for Cowork, Cursor announced a $29.3B valuation, GitHub Copilot CLI went GA with memory and sub-agents, and Notion shipped autonomous Custom Agents. A solo founder cannot track 17 competitors, research their latest changes, assess convergence risk, and generate sales battlecards -- all while shipping product. That's a full-time analyst role.

We built it as an AI function: a competitive-intelligence agent plus four cascading specialists (growth strategist, pricing strategist, deal architect, SEO specialist).

What it produced in one session:
- 2,843-word competitive intelligence report
- 7 Tier 0 competitors (platform threats) analyzed in overlap matrices
- 10 Tier 3 competitors (CaaS/full-stack) analyzed
- 3 new entrants identified
- 37 cited research sources with URLs
- 4 cascade outputs: content gap analysis, pricing matrix, 4 sales battlecards, SEO refresh queue

A competitive intelligence consultant prices this at $8,000-22,000 and 4-8 weeks.

The report directly changed the business validation verdict from PASS to CONDITIONAL PASS based on Tier 0 threat materialization. The analysis compounds -- the next quarterly scan starts from the existing framework, not from scratch.

## IndieHackers

**Title:** How I track 17 competitors as a solo founder without a full-time analyst

**Body:**

I build Soleur, a Company-as-a-Service platform. My competitive landscape is brutal -- I'm up against Anthropic, Cursor, GitHub Copilot, OpenAI, Notion AI, and a dozen others. The market moves weekly. In one 48-hour period last month, four major competitors shipped significant updates simultaneously.

A solo founder cannot track 17 competitors, research their changes, assess what converges with your product, and generate actionable outputs -- all while building. That's a full-time competitive intelligence analyst role. Consultants charge $8,000-22,000 for a comprehensive landscape analysis of this scope.

So I built it as an AI capability.

**The system:**

One competitive-intelligence agent handles the primary analysis, then four specialist agents cascade in parallel:
- Growth strategist: content gap analysis, 4-week content calendar
- Pricing strategist: competitive pricing matrix across all competitors
- Deal architect: sales battlecards for the 4 highest-overlap competitors
- SEO specialist: stale page detection, new comparison pages to create

**What came out of one session:**

A 2,843-word report with overlap matrices for 17 competitors across two threat tiers. 37 cited research sources with URLs. 3 new entrants identified and categorized. 6 prioritized recommendations. Plus the four cascade outputs -- pricing matrix, 4 battlecards, content strategy, SEO queue.

**What I didn't expect:**

The report directly changed my business validation verdict. I had assessed competitive risk as PASS. The intelligence report surfaced enough Tier 0 threat materialization (platform-native competitors building features that overlap with my core value proposition) to downgrade it to CONDITIONAL PASS. The system caught a strategic risk I had rationalized away.

That's the difference between a report and a function. A consultant gives you a PDF. A persistent competitive intelligence capability changes your strategic decisions in real time.

The next quarterly scan starts from the existing framework. The analysis compounds.

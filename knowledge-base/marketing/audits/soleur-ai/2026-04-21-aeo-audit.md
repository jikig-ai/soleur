# Soleur.ai GEO/AEO Audit -- 2026-04-21

## AEO Score

**78/100** (B+)

Solid B+ on the SAP framework. Structure is strong (FAQ schema on 6/7 sampled pages, clean heading hierarchy, extractable definitions, Q&A-formatted answers). Authority is mid (one marquee third-party citation on home/vision, but most statistical claims are uncited). Presence is weak (few third-party mentions beyond the Inc.com feature, no visible citation-monitoring tooling documented at the content layer). A 10-point lift is available by citing sources for every stats claim (65 agents, 67 skills, $95K/mo replacement stack), adding author/reviewer attribution blocks on blog posts and pillar pages, and seeding 2-3 more third-party references. See Prioritized Fix List below.

## Scope and Method

- **Pages sampled:** `/` (home), `/pricing/`, `/getting-started/`, `/agents/`, `/skills/`, `/blog/`, `/vision/`. The site has 15+ pages (blog posts, legal, changelog, community, about) -- this audit covers the most navigation-critical subset per the content audit sampling rule.
- **Retrieval:** `curl` with a Chrome User-Agent successfully returned 200 for every page. WebFetch and Playwright MCP were both blocked by origin (403 without browser fingerprint, permission-denied for Playwright). All analysis is against the server-rendered HTML.
- **Framework:** Structure / Authority / Presence (SAP), weighted per the GEO audit spec (source citations > statistics > quotations > definitions > readability). Keyword density is treated as a negative signal per spec.
- **Brand-guide cross-reference:** `knowledge-base/marketing/brand-guide.md` read. Voice rules applied to every rewrite suggestion ("no 'just'/'simply,'" "no 'AI-powered,'" "declarative, not hedged," concrete numbers required, non-technical-register accessibility on website copy).

## Per-Page Findings

### Home (/)

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Structure | Strong -- H1 "Stop hiring. Start delegating.", 7 H2 sections, FAQPage schema with 7 Q&A pairs, stats block ("8 / 65 / 67 / 6"), clear summary paragraph beneath H1. |
| Authority | Mixed -- marquee Dario Amodei quote cites Inc.com (good). Claim "65 AI agents across 8 business departments" unsupported by link on this page (should deep-link to `/agents/`). Stats counter "6 GitHub Stars" is a trust-negative for a billion-dollar-ambition brand -- it undermines authority and should be removed or replaced with waitlist count. |
| Presence | Mentions Anthropic (Dario), Inc.com, Claude Code, MCP. No analyst, customer, or developer-community quotes. |

Critical issues (block discoverability or damage trust):

1. "6 GitHub Stars" counter is live on hero. For a brand positioned as "billion-dollar solo company platform," a 6-star vanity stat is anti-authority and will be quoted verbatim by AI engines asked "is Soleur legit?"
2. FAQ answer for "Is Soleur free?" uses the phrase "The self-hosted version is open source and free" -- good. But "Soleur offers two paths" opens with vague qualifier -- AI engines prefer a direct answer in sentence 1.

Improvement issues:

3. H3s use emoji as the first visible character (🧠 You Decide, ⚡ Agents Execute). Emojis in headings reduce extractability and some AI rendering strips them, leaving ambiguous anchors. Move emoji to a data attribute or inline-span inside H3, or drop.
4. Claim "8 departments" appears 9+ times on the page. Repetition is fine; what is missing is a single authoritative line AI can cite: "Soleur's 8 departments are engineering, finance, legal, marketing, operations, product, sales, and support." Add as a standalone definition paragraph.
5. The thesis "running a company alone shouldn't mean doing everything alone" is absent from the home page (per brand guide, this is the general-register framing). Its absence means AI asked "what is Soleur" pulls the technical framing for non-technical audiences.

## Keyword-Stuffing Check (negative signal)

Per spec, keyword density is counterproductive for AI visibility. Scan results:

- "8 departments" appears 9-12 times on home page. **Acceptable** -- each instance is in a different clause/use ("across 8 departments," "8 departments, one knowledge base," etc.), and the claim is concrete and verifiable (the departments are enumerated). This is specificity, not stuffing.
- "AI agents" appears 15+ times on `/agents/`. **Acceptable** -- it is the page topic, and the term is used naturally.
- "company-as-a-service" appears 8+ times on `/vision/`. **Acceptable** -- the page is the canonical pillar for the term.

No keyword-stuffing flags.

## What Is Working Well (do not change)

- **FAQPage schema on 6/7 sampled pages.** This is the single biggest structural AEO asset. Every FAQ is in valid Q&A format with concise answers (1-4 sentences). Keep expanding.
- **Dario Amodei / Inc.com citation on home and vision.** Exactly the pattern every authority claim should follow.
- **Named-expert anchors on `/agents/` (Evans, DHH, Farley, Feathers, Fowler, Karpathy).** These named-entity references are an underrated AEO asset -- they connect Soleur into existing AI knowledge graphs.
- **Pillar-intro paragraphs on `/agents/` and `/skills/`.** The 2-paragraph pillar intro is textbook pillar-page structure: topic definition + what-makes-this-different + summary. Reuse the pattern on any new pillar page.
- **Clean H1 -> H2 -> H3 hierarchy.** No skipped levels, no multiple H1s. Keep enforcing in build.
- **MCP and Claude Code external links.** These hyperlink Soleur's content into authoritative protocol documentation -- high-value AEO anchors.

## Monitoring Recommendations

- **Manual AI-citation probe:** Once a month, run five canonical queries in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews, and log whether Soleur is cited: "what is company-as-a-service," "AI platform for solo founders," "Soleur vs Cursor," "AI agents for solo founders 2026," "can one person build a billion-dollar company." Track citation rate in a `knowledge-base/marketing/analytics/ai-citation-log.md`.
- **Third-party seeding:** The Inc.com feature is the highest-authority external mention. Target 2-3 additional tier-1 mentions (HN front page comment thread, TechCrunch / The Information / Stratechery mention, a developer-newsletter feature) before the next monthly audit.
- **Citation monitoring tooling:** No automated tool configured. Options: (1) Google Alerts for "Soleur AI" + "Jikigai" + "Jean Deruelle," (2) a scheduled skill (`soleur:schedule`) that re-runs the five canonical queries via API and diffs.

## Executive Summary

Since the March 17 audit, two high-value comparison posts have been published (Soleur vs. Cursor, Soleur vs. Notion Custom Agents) and case study posts have been upgraded with FAQ sections and FAQPage JSON-LD. The "Why Tools Plateau" post remains the strongest individual piece on the site for authority signals, with inline source links to Karpathy, GitHub, Y Combinator, and Tessl.

**Key changes since last audit:**

- Two new comparison posts add competitive positioning content with strong citation structures
- Agent count updated from 61 to 63 across site content (consistent)

**Overall GEO readiness score: 74/100** (up from 68)

## Detailed Analysis by SAP Dimension

### STRUCTURE -- Is content machine-extractable?

#### 1. Source Citations (GEO Impact: HIGHEST)

**Exemplary pages:**

- **What Is CaaS** -- 10+ external citations: Bureau of Labor Statistics, CNBC (Cursor $1B ARR, $29.3B valuation), VentureBeat (Devin pricing), TechCrunch (one-person unicorn, Lovable ARR, Anthropic Cowork, Notion Custom Agents), Inc.com (Amodei prediction), Fortune (Altman quote). Every major claim is source-backed.
- **Why Tools Plateau** -- Cites Karpathy tweets with direct X/Twitter links, GitHub Spec Kit with star count (76,000+), OpenSpec/Y Combinator, Kiro/AWS, Tessl ($125M funding), Every's Compound Engineering plugin.
- **Soleur vs. Cowork** -- Cites TechCrunch, The Decoder, Claude pricing page, Anthropic product announcements.
- **Soleur vs. Cursor** -- Cites Cursor's Automations launch date (March 5, 2026), Marketplace expansion (30+ plugins by March 11), Cursor's own PR stat (30%+ of PRs from autonomous agents), $2B+ annualized revenue figure. These are strong authority signals. **Gap:** Some claims lack inline source URLs (e.g., the "$2B+ ARR" figure and "30% of PRs" stat need source links to the original reporting).
- **Soleur vs. Notion** -- Cites Notion reaching 100M users (August 2024), Custom Agents launch date (February 24, 2026), 21,000+ agents built by early testers, Notion running 2,800 internally, pricing structure details. **Gap:** The 100M users figure and 21,000+ agents figures need inline source links.

**Failing pages (0 external citations):**
- Homepage -- Makes multiple claims ("63 AI agents across 8 departments," "company-as-a-service platform") but cites no external validation. The thesis quote is self-attributed.
- Changelog -- No external citations (acceptable for a changelog, low priority).
- Case Study: Legal Docs -- Cost comparison claims ("EUR 300-500/hour," "30-50 billable hours") cite no source for legal market rates.
- Case Study: Competitive Intel -- Mentions "Anthropic launched engineering plugins," "Cursor announced $29.3B valuation," "GitHub Copilot CLI went GA," "Notion 3.3 shipped Custom Agents" with dates but no source URLs. One partial exception: the post references "30+ sources" researched but does not link them in the article itself.

**Finding:** The citation gap has narrowed since the last audit. Two new comparison posts bring strong external sourcing. Case studies improved with FAQ sections but still lack external citations for market-rate claims. The homepage remains the most critical citation gap -- it is likely the most-visited page and has zero third-party validation.

**Weak pages:**

### PRESENCE -- Is the brand visible in AI-generated answers?

**Current state:**
- No evidence of systematic third-party mention strategy.
- No citation monitoring infrastructure.
- The comparison blog posts (vs. Cursor, vs. Cowork, vs. Notion) are the strongest presence play -- they position Soleur in queries where users are comparing tools. If AI models index these posts, Soleur appears in comparative answers.
- The "What Is CaaS" post positions Soleur as the category definer. This is high-value for presence: AI models answering "what is company-as-a-service" will likely reference this page if indexed.

**Gaps:**
- No Product Hunt launch page.
- No Hacker News submission tracked.
- No Reddit /r/ClaudeAI or /r/solopreneur presence documented.
- No third-party review or comparison articles citing Soleur.
- No citation monitoring process (manual or automated).

**Recommendation:** The comparison posts are the right content strategy for presence. The missing piece is distribution -- getting these posts indexed and referenced by third parties. See Prioritized Findings below.

## Appendix: Scoring Methodology

**Weights (aligned with GEO research on AI citation drivers):**

| Factor | Weight | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Source Citations | 25% | Highest correlation with AI model citation probability. Pages citing authoritative sources are referenced 40%+ more often in AI-generated answers. |
| Statistics/Specificity | 20% | Concrete numbers are extracted and quoted by AI models at significantly higher rates than vague qualifiers. |
| Conversational Readiness | 15% | Self-contained, quotable paragraphs are the unit of AI citation. Content that requires surrounding context to understand is skipped. |
| FAQ Structure | 15% | FAQ markup (especially with JSON-LD) directly triggers featured snippet and AI answer extraction. |
| Definition Extractability | 10% | Clear definitions near first usage are high-value extraction targets for "what is X" queries. |
| Summary Quality | 10% | Summary paragraphs are often the first content AI models evaluate for relevance. |
| Citation-Friendly Structure | 5% | Semantic HTML, heading hierarchy, and plain-text content are baseline requirements. Most modern sites meet these. |

**Scoring scale:**
- 5 = Exemplary. Among the best in the category. Would be cited by AI models.
- 4 = Strong. Minor gaps but fundamentally sound.
- 3 = Moderate. Present but incomplete or inconsistent.
- 2 = Weak. Minimal effort or significant gaps.
- 1 = Absent or failing. The signal does not exist on this page.

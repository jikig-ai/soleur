## Warnings

### 1. About page lacks author credentials and bio

**Severity:** Medium
**Impact:** E-E-A-T signals across all blog posts and the entire site
**Category:** E-E-A-T Signals

The About page (`/about/`) identifies Jean Deruelle as "Founder" but provides no professional background, expertise indicators, education, or track record. Google's helpful content system evaluates whether content is created by someone with demonstrable expertise. For a site publishing thought leadership on AI agents, agentic engineering, and business strategy, the absence of any credentials or professional narrative is a missed opportunity.

The ProfilePage JSON-LD correctly identifies the author and links to social profiles, but the visible page content is minimal (3 short paragraphs).

**Recommendation:** Add 2-3 paragraphs of professional background to the About page. Include relevant experience, technical credentials, and the founding story with enough detail to establish domain expertise. This does not need to be a full biography -- a concise professional narrative strengthens every blog post's E-E-A-T signal because all posts link to this page via `rel="author"`.

**File:** `plugins/soleur/docs/pages/about.njk`

### 2. Only 1 of 16 blog posts has a dateModified distinct from datePublished

**Severity:** Medium-Low
**Impact:** Blog post freshness signals
**Category:** E-E-A-T Signals

Only `why-most-agentic-tools-plateau.md` has an `updated` field in its frontmatter. For the other 15 blog posts, the BlogPosting JSON-LD `dateModified` equals `datePublished` because the template uses `{{ updated | dateToRfc3339 if updated else date | dateToRfc3339 }}`. While this is technically correct (the posts have not been updated), Google uses dateModified as a freshness signal. As posts age, adding an `updated` field when substantive edits are made will signal ongoing content maintenance.

**Recommendation:** No immediate action needed. Establish a practice of adding `updated: YYYY-MM-DD` to blog post frontmatter when making substantive content updates. Consider a quarterly content review cycle for evergreen posts (what-is-company-as-a-service, ai-agents-for-solo-founders) that would benefit from refreshed statistics or updated competitive landscape information.

**Files:** All files in `plugins/soleur/docs/blog/`

### 3. Heading hierarchy issues on homepage and getting-started page

**Severity:** Low-Medium
**Impact:** Content structure signals for crawlers and screen readers
**Category:** Content Quality

Two pages have heading hierarchy issues:

**Homepage (`index.njk`):** Line 105 has `<h3 class="feature-grid-sublabel">The Workflow</h3>` used as a visual label for the workflow section, followed by h3 headings for each workflow step (Think, Plan, Build, Review, Ship, Compound). The label and its children are at the same heading level, which makes the document outline ambiguous. The workflow section sits under the h2 "8 departments. One knowledge base. Your expertise, amplified." which is semantically about departments, not the workflow.

**Getting-started page (`pages/getting-started.njk`):** After the h2 "Installation" section, four h3 headings ("The Workflow", "Commands", "Example Workflows", "Learn More") function as top-level page sections but are coded as h3. They should be h2 to reflect their actual document structure.

**Recommendation:**
- Homepage: Promote "The Workflow" to h2 and keep the individual workflow steps as h3 underneath it, OR wrap the workflow section in its own h2 container.
- Getting-started: Promote "The Workflow", "Commands", "Example Workflows", and "Learn More" from h3 to h2.

**Files:**
- `plugins/soleur/docs/index.njk` (line 105)
- `plugins/soleur/docs/pages/getting-started.njk`

### 4. `<base href="/">` tag present on all pages

**Severity:** Low
**Impact:** Edge-case crawler issues
**Category:** Technical SEO

The `<base href="/">` tag in `base.njk` (line 87) sets the base URL for all relative links. While this works correctly for this site, it is a legacy pattern that can cause unexpected behavior with fragment identifiers, relative URLs in dynamically injected content, and some crawler implementations. Since all navigation links already use root-relative paths (e.g., `href="getting-started/"`) and canonical URLs are absolute, the `<base>` tag is redundant.

**Recommendation:** Remove `<base href="/">` from `base.njk` and verify all relative links still resolve correctly. Low priority -- this is unlikely to cause actual indexing issues.

**File:** `plugins/soleur/docs/_includes/base.njk` (line 87)

### 5. `<html lang="en">` does not match og:locale and JSON-LD inLanguage

**Severity:** Low
**Impact:** Minor inconsistency signal
**Category:** Technical SEO

The HTML lang attribute is `en` while og:locale is `en_US` and all JSON-LD inLanguage values are `en-US`. While `en` is a valid and broad language tag, aligning it with the more specific `en-US` used elsewhere would improve consistency. Search engines handle this gracefully, but consistency across all language declarations is a best practice.

**Recommendation:** Change `<html lang="en">` to `<html lang="en-US">` in `base.njk`.

**File:** `plugins/soleur/docs/_includes/base.njk` (line 2)

### 6. All blog posts use the same generic OG image

**Severity:** Low
**Impact:** Social sharing differentiation
**Category:** Meta Tags

All blog posts use the default `og-image.png` for their og:image and twitter:image. When multiple blog posts are shared in the same social feed or chat thread, they appear identical. The pricing page already demonstrates per-page OG image support (`ogImage: pricing-og.png` in frontmatter), so the infrastructure exists.

**Recommendation:** Create unique OG images for high-traffic blog posts (at minimum: what-is-company-as-a-service, ai-agents-for-solo-founders, vibe-coding-vs-agentic-engineering) using a consistent template. These posts are most likely to be shared on social media and would benefit from visual differentiation. Low priority -- the current generic image is functional and branded.

**Files:** Blog post frontmatter in `plugins/soleur/docs/blog/`

### 7. No visible contact information on main site pages

**Severity:** Low
**Impact:** Trust signals for E-E-A-T
**Category:** E-E-A-T Signals

The site footer links to Legal, Privacy Policy, and Terms of Service, and the About page links to social profiles. However, no contact email address or physical address is visible on the main site pages (about, community, or a dedicated contact page). Contact information may exist within legal documents, but surfacing it on a main navigation page strengthens trust signals.

**Recommendation:** Add a contact email (or contact form) to the About or Community page. Even a simple "Contact: hello@soleur.ai" or equivalent strengthens trust signals for both users and search engine quality evaluators.

## Executive Summary

The soleur.ai site has strong foundational content with a clear value proposition and consistent messaging. The site does well in several areas:

- **Consistent terminology:** "Company-as-a-Service," "compounding knowledge base," "65 agents, 8 departments" appear throughout, reinforcing brand identity.
- **FAQ sections on every page:** Excellent for AEO/GEO -- provides extractable Q&A pairs for AI engines.
- **Comparison posts are thorough:** The "Soleur vs. X" series covers competitors with data-backed claims and structured comparison tables.
- **Case studies provide proof:** Five case studies with concrete outputs (word counts, document counts, cost comparisons).

Key problems identified:

1. **Homepage H1 is aspirational, not keyword-aligned.** "Build a Billion-Dollar Company. Alone." targets no search query. The page title "Stop Hiring. Start Delegating." is a tagline, not a search-intent title.
2. **Missing target keywords across core pages.** Terms like "AI business automation," "one-person company tools," "AI startup tools," and "Claude Code plugin" are absent or underused.
3. **Blog index has zero categorization.** No tags, categories, or topic clusters -- all 16 posts listed chronologically. Hurts internal linking and topic authority.
4. **Thin content on Community and Changelog pages.** Community page has ~200 words of original content. Changelog is auto-generated with no narrative.
5. **No author bylines on blog posts.** Hurts E-E-A-T signals. The About page mentions Jean Deruelle but blog posts have no visible attribution.
6. **Case studies use identical heading structure.** All five use "The AI Approach / The Result / The Cost Comparison / The Compound Effect" -- monotonous for crawlers expecting unique heading hierarchies.
7. **Pricing page has no schema-friendly pricing summary.** Tiers are described but not in a format easily extractable by AI engines.

## Rewrite Suggestions

### RS1: Homepage Title Tag

**Current:** "Soleur - Stop Hiring. Start Delegating."
**Suggested:** "Soleur -- Company-as-a-Service Platform | AI Agents for Solo Founders"
**Rationale:** Places the category term "Company-as-a-Service" and primary audience "Solo Founders" in the title tag where search engines weight them most. Keeps brand name first.

### RS2: Homepage H1

**Current:** "Build a Billion-Dollar Company. Alone."
**Suggested:** "The AI Organization for Solo Founders"
**Rationale:** Targets "AI organization" and "solo founders" -- both high-value search terms -- while keeping the direct, confident tone. The billion-dollar claim can remain in subheading copy.

### RS3: Homepage Meta Description

**Current:** "One command center. 8 departments. AI agents that remember everything about your business."
**Suggested:** "Deploy 65 AI agents across 8 business departments -- engineering, marketing, legal, finance, sales, operations, product, and support. A compounding knowledge base that remembers everything about your company. Free and open source."
**Rationale:** Adds keyword density for "AI agents," "business departments," specific department names, "compounding knowledge base," and "free and open source" -- all high-intent search terms. 155 characters for SERP display.

### RS4: About Page H1

**Current:** "About"
**Suggested:** "About Soleur: Company-as-a-Service for Solo Founders"
**Rationale:** Adds brand name and category term to H1 for navigational queries.

### RS5: Skills Page H1

**Current:** "Agentic Engineering Skills"
**Suggested:** "Soleur Skills: 64 Workflow Automations Across 8 Departments"
**Rationale:** Corrects the H1/content mismatch. "Workflow automations" is a more searchable term than "agentic engineering skills" for non-technical visitors.

### RS6: Blog Index -- Add Topic Sections

**Current:** Chronological list of 16 posts.
**Suggested:** Group under topic headings:
- **What is Company-as-a-Service?** (pillar: "What Is Company-as-a-Service?", cluster: "AI Agents for Solo Founders")
- **Soleur vs. Competitors** (5 comparison posts)
- **Case Studies** (5 case studies)
- **Engineering Deep Dives** (vibe coding, agentic tools plateau, credential helper, codebase connection)

**Rationale:** Topic clustering signals authority to search engines and helps users find content by intent. Each cluster can become a dedicated landing page in future.

### RS7: Case Study Title Pattern

**Current:** "From Scattered Positioning to a Full Brand Guide in Two Sessions"
**Suggested:** "How Soleur Built a Full Brand Guide in Two Sessions"
**Rationale:** Includes "Soleur" for branded search. Pattern "How Soleur [achieved X]" is more search-friendly and clarifies the tool used.

Apply same pattern to other case studies:
- "How Soleur Validated a Business Idea with AI Gates"
- "How Soleur Tracked 17 Competitors with AI Battlecards"
- "How Soleur Generated 9 Legal Documents in Days"
- "How Soleur Built an Operations Department for One Person"

### RS8: Changelog Meta Description

**Current:** (none detected)
**Suggested:** "All notable changes to Soleur -- new agents, skills, fixes, and platform updates. Semantic versioning with continuous deployment."
**Rationale:** Targets "Soleur changelog" and "Soleur updates" navigational queries.

### RS9: Community Page -- Add Depth

**Current:** ~200 words, mostly links to external platforms.
**Suggested additions:**
- Community stats (Discord member count, GitHub stars, contributor count)
- Featured community contributions or testimonials
- "Getting Involved" guide with specific first-contribution suggestions
- Embedded recent Discord activity or GitHub activity feed

**Rationale:** Thin pages hurt domain authority. Community pages with stats and activity demonstrate a live project.

**Current example (Homepage):** "Soleur deploys 65 AI agents across 8 business departments -- engineering, marketing, legal, finance, operations, product, sales, and support -- giving a single founder the capacity of a full organization."
**Assessment:** This answer is already concise (1 sentence). KEEP.

## Methodology

**Pages audited:** 26 of 36 total pages in sitemap. The 10 legal sub-pages (terms, privacy policy, cookie policy, GDPR, acceptable use, data protection, disclaimer, individual CLA, corporate CLA) were excluded from detailed analysis as they serve compliance purposes with minimal SEO value. The legal index page was audited.

**Assessment criteria:**
- **Keyword alignment:** Are target keywords present in title tag, H1, meta description, first paragraph, and subheadings?
- **Search intent match:** Does the page content match what a user searching the target keyword would expect to find?
- **Readability:** Is the content scannable, well-structured, and at an appropriate reading level for the target audience (technical solo founders)?
- **Content depth:** Is the word count and detail level sufficient to demonstrate expertise on the topic?
- **E-E-A-T signals:** Does the content demonstrate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness?

**Data collection:** All pages fetched via WebFetch on 2026-04-13. Content extracted including titles, meta descriptions, headings, body text, FAQ sections, and calls to action.

**Brand guide status:** No brand guide found. Voice patterns inferred from existing content: direct, technically precise, confident without hyperbole, founder-to-founder tone, minimal jargon outside established terms (CaaS, agentic engineering).

**Limitations:**
- JavaScript-rendered content may not have been fully captured by the fetch tool
- Internal linking structure was assessed from content mentions, not a full crawl
- Search volume data was not available for keyword gap analysis -- relevance assessed qualitatively
- Mobile rendering and Core Web Vitals not assessed (outside content audit scope)

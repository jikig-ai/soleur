# GEO/AEO Content Audit Report -- soleur.ai

**Audit date:** 2026-03-17
**Framework:** Structure / Authority / Presence (SAP)
**Brand guide:** Present at `knowledge-base/marketing/brand-guide.md` (last reviewed 2026-03-02). Brand voice: ambitious-inspiring, bold, forward-looking, precise. Target audience: solo founders building at billion-dollar scale.
**Pages audited:** 15 (homepage, 6 nav pages, 1 getting-started page, 7 blog/case-study posts)

---

## Executive Summary

The site is **strong on GEO/AEO fundamentals for its blog content** and **weak on its catalog/navigation pages**. The three pillar blog posts (What Is CaaS, Why Tools Plateau, Soleur vs. Cowork) are among the best-structured AI-citable content in the agentic engineering space -- rich in source citations, concrete statistics, FAQ sections with JSON-LD, and self-contained quotable paragraphs. The five case study posts are solid but lack external citations and FAQ sections. The catalog pages (Agents, Skills, Vision, Community) are marketing-optimized but structurally invisible to AI engines: no FAQ sections, no definitions, no quotable summary paragraphs, and no citations.

**Overall GEO readiness score: 68/100**

## Detailed Analysis by SAP Dimension

### STRUCTURE -- Is content machine-extractable?

#### 1. Source Citations (GEO Impact: HIGHEST)

**Exemplary pages:**
- **What Is CaaS** -- 10+ external citations with inline links: Bureau of Labor Statistics, CNBC (Cursor $1B ARR), VentureBeat (Devin pricing), TechCrunch (one-person unicorn, Lovable ARR, Anthropic Cowork launch, Notion Custom Agents), Inc.com (Amodei prediction), Fortune (Altman quote). Every major claim is backed by a named source.
- **Why Tools Plateau** -- Cites Karpathy tweets (with direct X links), GitHub Spec Kit (star count), Y Combinator backing for OpenSpec, Tessl's $125M funding, Every's Compound Engineering plugin.
- **Soleur vs. Cowork** -- Cites TechCrunch, The Decoder, Claude pricing page.

**Failing pages (0 external citations):**
- Homepage, Getting Started, Agents, Skills, Vision, Community, Legal, Changelog, and all five case study posts.

**Finding:** The site has a stark binary split. Blog pillar content is citation-rich. Everything else has zero external citations.

**Recommendation (Critical):** Add at least 1-2 external citations to each high-traffic catalog page.

#### 2. Statistics and Specificity (GEO Impact: HIGH)

**Exemplary pages:**
- **What Is CaaS** -- "$1 billion in ARR," "$29.3 billion valuation," "$20 per month," "$200 million in ARR," "55 employees," "$345 million per employee."
- **Why Tools Plateau** -- "76,000 stars," "$125 million in venture funding," "10,000 specs," "30 to 96 percent reduction."
- **Case studies** -- "9 documents totaling 17,761 words," "3,627-word business validation document," "37 cited research sources."

**Weak pages:**
- **Vision** -- Uses vague qualifiers: "infinite leverage," "near-zero marginal cost," "non-linear rewards." No concrete numbers.
- **Community, Skills** -- No statistics at all.

#### 3. FAQ Structure (GEO Impact: HIGH)

**Exemplary:**
- **Homepage** -- 6 FAQ items with `<details>/<summary>` + FAQPage JSON-LD.
- **What Is CaaS** -- 5 FAQ items with both HTML and JSON-LD.
- **Soleur vs. Cowork** -- 5 FAQ items with both HTML and JSON-LD.

**Missing FAQ entirely:**
- Getting Started, Skills, Vision, Community, Legal, Changelog, and all 5 case study posts.

**Gaps:**
- **Agents page** -- Has 3 FAQ items but no JSON-LD.
- **Why Tools Plateau** -- Uses H3 headings for FAQ instead of `<details>/<summary>` and has no FAQPage JSON-LD.

#### 4. Conversational Readiness (GEO Impact: HIGH)

**Exemplary quotable paragraphs:**

From **What Is CaaS:**
> "Company-as-a-Service (CaaS) is a new category of platform where a single AI organization runs every department of a business -- engineering, marketing, legal, finance, operations, product, sales, and support."

From **Why Tools Plateau:**
> "Compound engineering is the practice of designing AI-assisted development systems where each unit of work makes subsequent work easier."

**Weak conversational readiness:**
- **Vision** -- Written in aspirational marketing language. AI models avoid quoting aspirational prose.
- **Community** -- Purely navigational. Nothing quotable.

#### 1. Expert Attribution

**Strong:**
- **What Is CaaS** attributes quotes to Dario Amodei, Sam Altman, Mike Krieger with source links.
- **Why Tools Plateau** attributes terms to Andrej Karpathy with source links.

**Weak:**
- No author attribution on any non-blog page.
- No "About" page exists.

#### 2. E-E-A-T Signals

**Strong:**
- **Experience:** "Designed, built, and shipped by Soleur -- using Soleur."
- **Expertise:** Blog posts demonstrate deep technical knowledge.
- **Authoritativeness:** Original data (plan-review reduction table) from production usage.
- **Trustworthiness:** Legal pages present, open-source, transparent pricing.

**Weak:**
- **Vision page** reads like a pitch deck, not an expert analysis.
- No "About" page with founder credentials.

# AEO/GEO Content Audit -- soleur.ai

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Audited by:** Content Strategy Agent (GEO/AEO)
**Framework:** Structure / Authority / Presence (SAP)
**Pages audited:** 15 (homepage + 7 nav pages + 7 blog posts)
**Brand guide:** Reviewed (`knowledge-base/marketing/brand-guide.md`, last updated 2026-02-21)

---

## Executive Summary

soleur.ai scores well on several GEO/AEO dimensions -- particularly FAQ structure, structured data markup, and entity clarity. The site's blog content is the strongest asset: two pillar articles ("What Is Company-as-a-Service?" and "Why Most Agentic Engineering Tools Plateau") demonstrate best-in-class citation density, statistical specificity, and conversational readiness. The homepage and Getting Started page are solid. The weakest pages are Vision, Community, Changelog, Skills, and the case study blog posts, which lack FAQ sections, source citations, or both.

**Overall AEO Grade: B+**

The site is ahead of most early-stage startup sites in GEO/AEO readiness. The primary gaps are:

1. **Vision page** has zero source citations, no FAQ, and no structured data beyond the base template -- it is the weakest page on the site for AI extractability.
2. **Skills page** has no FAQ section and no definitions of what "agentic engineering skills" are in a quotable format.
3. **Case study blog posts** (5 posts) lack FAQ sections and FAQPage schema markup, unlike the pillar articles.
4. **Community page** is thin content with no definitions, no FAQ, and no quotable answers.
5. **Changelog page** is auto-generated and structurally invisible to AI engines.

## Scoring Methodology

Each page is scored 1--10 on eight dimensions. Scores reflect how well the content performs for AI engine extraction and citation, not traditional SEO.

| Score | Meaning |

| 9--10 | Best practice. AI engines can extract, quote, and cite this content directly. |
| 7--8 | Good. Minor improvements needed. |
| 5--6 | Adequate. Functional but not optimized for AI extraction. |
| 3--4 | Weak. Significant gaps in extractability or authority signals. |
| 1--2 | Missing or counterproductive. |

## Detailed Per-Page Analysis

### 1. Homepage (`index.njk` -- soleur.ai/)

**Strengths:**
- FAQ section with 6 question-answer pairs covering the most important queries ("What is Soleur?", "What is company-as-a-service?", "How does Soleur differ from Cursor/Copilot?", "Is Soleur free?", "Who is Soleur for?", "How do I get started?")
- FAQPage schema markup (JSON-LD) matches the visible FAQ content
- SoftwareApplication schema with price, category, OS, and feature list
- Strong entity definition in the first paragraph of the hero subtitle
- Statistics strip with concrete numbers (departments, agents, skills)
- Clear product definition: "Not a copilot. Not an assistant. A full AI organization that reviews, plans, builds, remembers, and self-improves."
- Quotable thesis: "The first billion-dollar company run by one person isn't science fiction. It's an engineering problem. We're solving it."

**Weaknesses:**
- No source citations. Every claim is first-party. No external links to authoritative sources.
- No external validation (reviews, awards, usage statistics from third parties).
- Statistics use template variables ({{ stats.agents }}) -- rendered values are concrete, but the source is self-reported without third-party verification.
- The "This Is the Way" section label is a pop culture reference that adds no SEO or AEO value.
- No summary paragraph that AI models could extract as a standalone description of the product.

**Recommendations (Priority: Critical/Improvement):**

| Priority | Issue | Recommendation |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| Critical | No source citations on homepage | Add 2-3 external citations supporting key claims. Example: cite Dario Amodei's billion-dollar solopreneur prediction (already cited in blog posts) in the quote section or FAQ. |
| Critical | No standalone summary paragraph | Add a 2-3 sentence summary above the FAQ that defines Soleur in plain language, suitable for AI extraction. Currently the closest is the hero subtitle, but it is embedded in marketing markup. |
| Improvement | "This Is the Way" section label | Replace with a descriptive label like "How It Works" or "The Model" for better semantic clarity. |
| Improvement | No third-party authority signals | Add a "Built with" or "As seen in" reference if any external coverage exists. |

**Strengths:**

**Recommendations:**

| Priority | Issue | Recommendation |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| Improvement | No Organization schema | Add Organization schema to the base layout with name, url, logo, founder, foundingDate, and sameAs (linking to GitHub, Discord, X, LinkedIn, Bluesky). |

## Content Architecture Assessment

The site has a natural pillar/cluster structure emerging, though it is not explicitly linked:

**Pillar 1: "What Is Company-as-a-Service?"**
- Cluster: Business Validation case study (links back)
- Cluster: Legal Documents case study (links back)
- Cluster: Operations case study (links back)
- Cluster: Brand Guide case study (links back)

- Missing: The case studies do not consistently link back to the CaaS pillar or to each other.

**Pillar 2: "Why Most Agentic Engineering Tools Plateau"**
- Cluster: Getting Started page (natural link target)
- Cluster: Agents page (natural link target)
- Cluster: Skills page (natural link target)
- Missing: None of these core pages link back to the Plateau article.

**Pillar 3: "Soleur vs. Anthropic Cowork" (Comparison)**
- Standalone comparison piece. Could anchor a cluster of competitor comparisons.
- Missing: No "Soleur vs Cursor", "Soleur vs GitHub Copilot", or "Soleur vs Devin" comparison pages.

**Recommendation:** Explicitly build the internal linking structure. Each case study should link to the CaaS pillar article. Core pages (Getting Started, Agents, Skills) should link to the Plateau article. Create additional comparison pages to build out Pillar 3.

## Notes

- This audit was conducted from source files in the repository (`plugins/soleur/docs/`), not from the rendered site. Template variables ({{ stats.agents }}, {{ stats.departments }}, etc.) are assumed to render correctly. The live site (soleur.ai) returned HTTP 403 to automated fetch requests, which prevented direct verification of rendered output.
- The blog post template (`_includes/blog-post.njk`) automatically adds BlogPosting schema, Open Graph article tags, and author attribution. This is well-implemented infrastructure.
- The `llms.txt` file is a meaningful AEO differentiator -- most competitor sites do not have one.
- Legal pages were excluded from scoring as they serve a compliance function, not an AEO function.

# GEO/AEO Content Audit: soleur.ai

**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Auditor:** Content Strategy Agent (Claude Opus 4.7)
**Framework:** Structure / Authority / Presence (SAP)
**Scope:** 10 core pages fetched live (home, about, agents, skills, pricing, getting-started, vision, community, blog index, changelog) plus 1 representative blog post ("What Is Company-as-a-Service?"). Blog tier scores carried forward from the 2026-04-13 baseline where content has not materially shifted.
**Brand guide:** `knowledge-base/marketing/brand-guide.md` found and applied (voice: bold, declarative, precise; no hedging; define technical terms on first use for the general register).
**Collection method:** Direct HTTP fetch via curl (WebFetch returned 403 from the site's CDN; curl with a standard UA resolved 301→200 on www).

**Overall score: 72/100 (B-)**

---

## Executive Summary

Soleur.ai has moved from a C+ (66) on 2026-04-13 to a B- (72) on 2026-04-18, driven by three concrete content changes now visible on the live site:

1. The **homepage** opens with a canonical product definition ("Soleur is an open-source company-as-a-service platform that deploys 65 AI agents across 8 business departments — engineering, marketing, legal, finance, operations, product, sales, and support — giving a single founder the operational capacity of a full organization."). This closes the single largest AI-extractability gap identified in the prior audit.
2. The **about page** now carries a substantive founder bio (Jean Deruelle, 15+ years distributed-systems experience, founding context in early 2026, link to MCP and blog) and a dedicated "About Soleur" paragraph. Authority signal on this page moved from near-zero to moderate.
3. **Statistics are now consistent across core pages** — 65 agents, 8 departments, 66 skills appear on home, about, agents, skills, pricing, and getting-started with matching numbers. The prior audit flagged inconsistent counts as a quotation risk.

The remaining ceiling on the score is held down by (a) presence — zero third-party validation, no press/"as featured in" section, no visible citation-monitoring process, and (b) core-page citation density — home, skills, community, getting-started still carry 0–1 external citations while competing blog posts carry 5–8. The about page still has no FAQ section, which is a missed structural opportunity for founder/company queries. The core-vs-blog quality gap persists (core pages average ~55, strong blog posts 80–92), so AI engines will continue to prefer blog content for citation.

## Detailed Analysis

### 1. FAQ Structure & Schema — 88/A

**What's working**

- FAQPage schema on 9 of 10 sampled core pages (all except the blog index, where it is reasonable to omit).
- 42 total `"@type": "Question"` entries across core pages — high coverage.
- Questions match real search queries: "What is Soleur?", "What are Soleur agents?", "Why should I pay $49/mo when AI coding tools cost $20?", "Does Soleur work on Windows, Linux, and macOS?".
- Answers are mostly 1–3 sentences and self-contained.

**What's broken**

| Page | Issue | Severity |
|---|---|:---:|
| `/about/` | No FAQ section or FAQPage schema. This is the page AI engines probe for "who founded Soleur," "when was Soleur founded," "what is Soleur" queries. Adding 4–5 Q&As would be the single highest-ROI structural fix on the site. | **P0** |
| `/vision/` | FAQ Qs are self-referential ("What is the Soleur thesis?") rather than category-level ("What is company-as-a-service for solo founders?"). | P1 |
| `/changelog/` | 3 generic versioning questions; does not capture "what's new in Soleur," "Soleur release cadence" queries. | P2 |
| `/blog/` | No FAQ. Acceptable but misses capture for "Soleur blog topics," "agentic engineering articles." | P2 |

### 2. Source Citations — 65/C

**What's working**

- The representative blog post ("What Is Company-as-a-Service?") cites 13 distinct external authorities including BLS.gov, TechCrunch, CNBC, Fortune, VentureBeat, Inc, Notion's own release notes, and Karpathy X posts. This is a model page.
- Homepage and about carry the Amodei / Anthropic quote with a direct Inc.com citation — verifiable and well-chosen.
- Pricing page cites the US BLS median-compensation basis inline, supporting the $95,000/mo comparison. This is the strongest authority claim on any core page.
- Vision page has three citations (Inc.com, theleanstartup.com, Wikipedia "Culture series") — weak mix but present.

**What's broken**

| Page | External citations | Issue |
|---|---:|---|
| `/agents/` | 0 | Claims "65 agents," role definitions, and departmental coverage with no external validation. |
| `/skills/` | 0 | H1 is "Agentic Engineering Skills" but the term is neither defined nor cited to any external authority (Karpathy, who coined adjacent terms, is cited in the blog but absent here). |
| `/getting-started/` | 0 | Zero external refs for Claude Code, MCP, or Git — all are named in-page and could trivially be cited. |
| `/changelog/` | 1 (GitHub releases) | A changelog with one internal-org citation. Release notes could link to upstream Claude Code changelog, MCP spec versions, etc. |
| `/blog/` (index) | 0 | Expected, but even 2–3 external authority references in the intro blurb would help. |

**Pattern:** Core pages average **0.9 external citations per page**; the strong blog post averages **13**. AI engines citation-weight content by per-page external-citation density — the blog will continue to be cited over core pages by a wide margin.

### 3. Statistics & Specificity — 80/B

**What's working (improved since 2026-04-13)**

- 65 agents / 8 departments / 66 skills now appear **consistently** across home, about, agents, skills, pricing, getting-started. Prior audit flagged these as inconsistent (65 vs 63, 62 vs 64 skills).
- Pricing page remains statistics-dense: $18,000/mo CTO · $10,000/mo Marketing Director · $15,000/mo General Counsel · $15,000/mo CFO · $12,000/mo Sales Director · $8,000/mo Operations · $10,000/mo Product · $7,000/mo Support · Total $95,000/mo vs From $49/mo. Every figure is quotable.
- About page adds a specific founder statistic: "15+ years of experience building distributed systems and developer tools."
- Homepage now uses "70–80% probability" when citing Amodei — specific, not vague.

**What's still weak**

- `/skills/` lists 66 skills across 5 categories but **no performance metrics** (completion rates, time savings, usage frequency). Skills are a product differentiator; they should carry measurable outcomes.
- `/community/` has 464 words and **zero numbers**. No Discord member count, no GitHub star count, no contributor count, no issue-resolution time. This is the least specific page on the site.
- `/agents/` lists agents by department but no per-agent output metrics (e.g., "code-reviewer blocks N classes of regression," "copywriter produces content in X registers").
- Homepage uses `∞ Compounding Knowledge` as a stat. Infinity is not a citation target — replace with a concrete lower bound ("420+ PRs across 8 departments" as used in the brand guide's voice examples).

## Brand-Voice Alignment Notes

The rewrite recommendations above respect the brand guide's voice rules:

- **Declarative, no hedging.** Avoid "might," "could," "potentially" in added definitions and summaries.
- **No "AI-powered" or "leverage AI"** — these are banned per the brand guide. Use "AI organization," "AI agents," or "full-stack AI."
- **No "just," "simply," "assistant," "copilot," "terminal-first," or "plugin" in public-facing content** (exception: CLI commands).
- **Trust scaffolding:** Every added mention of autonomy must pair with "human-in-the-loop" or "your review before execution" per the 2026-03-26 synthetic-research review finding.
- **Technical vs general register:** Homepage and about use the general register; agents, skills, changelog can carry the technical register. Do not mix on a single page.
- **Number preference:** Concrete counts ("65 agents," "8 departments," "66 skills," "420+ PRs") over vague qualifiers. This aligns with the brand guide's "Use concrete numbers when available."

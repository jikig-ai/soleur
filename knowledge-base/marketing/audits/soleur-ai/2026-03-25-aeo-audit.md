## Detailed Analysis by SAP Dimension

### STRUCTURE -- Is content machine-extractable?

#### 1. Source Citations (GEO Impact: HIGHEST)

Source citations are the single most important factor for AI engine citation probability. Pages with inline external citations are 2-3x more likely to be referenced by generative AI models.

**Exemplary pages (score 5):**

- **What Is CaaS** -- 10+ external citations: Bureau of Labor Statistics, CNBC (Cursor $1B ARR, $29.3B valuation), VentureBeat (Devin pricing), TechCrunch (one-person unicorn, Lovable ARR, Anthropic Cowork, Notion Custom Agents), Inc.com (Amodei 70-80% prediction, Krieger Instagram quote), Fortune (Altman quote). Every major claim is source-backed. This remains the gold standard for the site.
- **Why Tools Plateau** -- Cites Karpathy tweets with direct X links, GitHub Spec Kit with star count (76,000+), OpenSpec/Y Combinator, Kiro/AWS, Tessl ($125M funding), Every's Compound Engineering. The plan review reduction table (30-96%) is self-cited from real project data with a clear methodology narrative.
- **Soleur vs. Cowork** -- Cites TechCrunch (Anthropic enterprise agents, Feb 24 2026), The Decoder (Microsoft Copilot Cowork, March 9 2026), Claude pricing page, Anthropic product announcements. Four named external references listed at article end.

**Moderate pages (score 3-4):**

- **Soleur vs. Cursor** (4) -- Cites Cursor Automations launch date, Marketplace expansion, "30%+ of PRs" stat, "$2B+" revenue figure. **Gap:** The $2B ARR and 30% PR stats lack inline source URLs pointing to the original reporting. Adding "[source]" hyperlinks would lift this to 5.
- **Soleur vs. Notion** (4) -- Cites 100M users, Custom Agents launch, 21,000+ agents built, 2,800 internal agents. **Gap:** Same issue -- figures cited without inline links to source articles.
- **AI Agents for Solo Founders** (3) -- This is a 3,000+ word definitive guide that makes zero external citations. No market data, no third-party statistics, no named sources. Every claim is internally derived. This is a significant gap for a pillar-intent page targeting the keyword "AI agents for solo founders." The CaaS pillar post achieves its 5/5 score precisely because it backs claims with named sources -- this guide should follow the same pattern.
- **Vibe Coding vs AE** (3) -- Attributes "vibe coding" to Karpathy (Feb 2025) and "agentic engineering" to Karpathy (Feb 2026) but does not link to the original posts. No other external citations.
- **Case Study: Business Validation** (2) -- References "Naval Ravikant, Amodei predictions, solo founder growth statistics" but does not provide source links.
- **Case Study: Competitive Intel** (2) -- References "Anthropic launched engineering plugins," "Cursor announced $29.3B valuation" with dates but no source URLs. Mentions "30+ sources" researched but does not reproduce them.

**Failing pages (score 1 -- zero external citations):**

| Page | Claims Made Without Sources |
|------|----------------------------|
| Homepage | "63 AI agents," "company-as-a-service platform," thesis quote (self-attributed) |
| Getting Started | "63 agents across engineering, finance..." -- no validation |
| Agents | "63 AI agents across 8 business departments" -- no external validation |
| Skills | "59 workflow skills" -- no external validation |
| Pricing | Cost comparison table (Cursor $20/mo, Devin $20/mo, Copilot $10-39/mo) cites no sources for competitor pricing |
| Vision | "model-agnostic orchestration," "multiplanetary companies" -- aspirational claims with no sourcing |
| Community | No citations of any kind |
| Case Study: Brand Guide | "$5,000-15,000 for a brand guide" -- no source for agency rates |
| Case Study: Legal Docs | "EUR 300-500/hour," "30-50 billable hours" -- no source for legal market rates |
| Case Study: Operations | "$100-250/hour," "15-25 hour engagement" -- no source for consultant rates |

**Critical finding (C1):** 11 of 18 pages have zero or minimal external source citations. The homepage -- the most visited page -- has zero. This is the single most impactful deficiency for AI citation probability.

**Recommendation:** Add 2-3 inline source citations to every core page. Priority order: (1) Homepage -- add TechCrunch one-person unicorn quote, Amodei prediction, or Cursor/Lovable revenue figures as market context. (2) Pricing page -- add source links for competitor pricing claims. (3) Case studies -- add source links for market-rate cost claims (legal fees, agency rates, consultant rates).

#### 2. Statistics and Specificity (GEO Impact: HIGH)

AI models preferentially cite content with concrete numbers over vague qualifiers. "63 agents across 8 departments" is citable; "many agents across multiple departments" is not.

**Exemplary pages (score 5):**

- **What Is CaaS** -- "$1 billion in ARR," "$29.3 billion valuation," "$200 million in ARR," "$6.6 billion valuation," "55 employees," "$345 million per employee," "70-80% probability." The densest statistical content on the site.
- **Soleur vs. Cursor** -- "$2B+ annualized revenue," "30+ Marketplace plugins," "30%+ of PRs," exact pricing tiers ($0, $20, $60, $200). The comparison table is ideal for AI extraction.
- **Soleur vs. Cowork** -- Claude Pro $20/mo, Team $25/seat/mo, Copilot Cowork $30/user/mo, M365 E7 $99/user/mo. Pricing specificity is strong.
- **Pricing** -- Exact pricing for Soleur ($0), Open Source features (63 agents, 59 skills), Hosted Pro ($49/mo + 10% revenue share). Competitor comparison table with prices. Dense and specific.
- **Case Study: Legal Docs** -- "9 documents totaling 17,761 words across 1,872 lines," "EUR 9,000-25,000," "30-50 billable hours." Highly specific.
- **Case Study: Competitive Intel** -- "2,843-word report," "7 Tier 0 competitors," "10 Tier 3 competitors," "37 cited research sources," "$6,000-18,000."
- **Why Tools Plateau** -- "76,000 stars," "$125 million in venture funding," "10,000 specs." Plan review reduction table with exact percentages.

**Moderate pages (score 3-4):**

- **Homepage** (4) -- "8 Departments," "63 AI Agents," "59 Skills" are concrete. The infinity symbol for "Compounding Knowledge" is not parseable by AI models -- this should be replaced with a concrete number (e.g., "500+ learnings captured" or "400+ merged PRs").
- **Agents** (4) -- Lists 63 agents by name across 8 departments. The count is specific. But there are no performance statistics, usage numbers, or outcomes data.
- **AI Agents Guide** (3) -- References "eight domains" and "eight distinct domains" but provides no market statistics, no revenue figures, no third-party numbers. A definitive guide without external data points reads as opinion, not authority.
- **Getting Started** (3) -- "63 agents," "59 skills," "3 commands." Concrete but narrow.
- **Skills** (3) -- "59 workflow skills." Lists categories and counts. No outcome statistics.

**Weak pages (score 1-2):**

- **Vibe Coding vs AE** (2) -- Attributes concepts to Karpathy with dates but provides no market data, no adoption statistics, no performance benchmarks.
- **Vision** (2) -- Uses vague qualifiers: "near-zero marginal cost," "non-linear rewards," "billion-dollar enterprise." The milestone descriptions (hardware companies, multiplanetary companies) are aspirational without grounding data.
- **Community** (1) -- No statistics at all. No member counts, no activity metrics, no community growth data.

**Finding (I1):** The site's statistical density is bimodal -- blog posts are rich with numbers, catalog pages are sparse. Adding 2-3 statistics to each catalog page would significantly improve their AI citation potential.

**Recommendation:** (1) Replace the infinity symbol on the homepage with a concrete metric. (2) Add community statistics to the Community page (Discord member count, GitHub stars, contributor count). (3) Add outcome statistics to the Agents page (e.g., "26 engineering review agents covering security, performance, architecture, and code quality"). (4) Add statistics to the Vision page grounding the milestones (e.g., AI market size projections from credible sources).

#### 3. Conversational Readiness (GEO Impact: HIGH)

Conversational readiness measures whether AI models can directly extract and quote content in natural-language answers. Self-contained paragraphs that answer implicit questions score highest.

**Exemplary quotable paragraphs (score 5):**

From **What Is CaaS:**
> "Company-as-a-Service (CaaS) is a new category of platform where a single AI organization runs every department of a business -- engineering, marketing, legal, finance, operations, product, sales, and support."

From **AI Agents Guide:**
> "A chatbot answers a question. An AI agent completes a task."

> "Four properties define a true agent: Goal-orientation, Tool use, Memory, and Accountability."

From **Why Tools Plateau:**
> "Compound engineering is the practice of designing AI-assisted development systems where each unit of work makes subsequent work easier."

From **Homepage FAQ:**
> "Soleur is a company-as-a-service platform delivered as a Claude Code plugin. It deploys 63 AI agents across 8 business departments."

**Moderate conversational readiness (score 3-4):**

- **Pricing** (4) -- FAQ answers are self-contained and quotable: "Yes. The Soleur plugin is open source and free to install."
- **Getting Started** (4) -- Installation instructions are clear and extractable. FAQ answers are self-contained.
- **Agents** (3) -- The overview describes agentic engineering but the bulk of the page is a list of agent names without explanatory context. An AI model asked "what are Soleur's engineering agents?" would get names but not what they do.
- **Skills** (3) -- Similar issue: skill names listed without descriptions of what each skill accomplishes.
- **Vision** (3) -- The "Core Value Proposition" section has quotable paragraphs but the milestone descriptions mix aspirational framing with concrete claims in a way that reduces quotability.

**Weak conversational readiness (score 2):**

- **Community** (2) -- "Connect, contribute, and get help" is not quotable. The page reads as a link hub, not as content an AI model would cite.

**Finding (I2):** Catalog pages (Agents, Skills) list items without explanatory descriptions. AI models need sentences, not just lists. An agent name like "security-sentinel" is not quotable; "The security-sentinel agent performs automated security review of code changes, checking for common vulnerabilities, dependency risks, and authentication flaws" is.

**Recommendation:** Add 1-2 sentence descriptions to at least the top 10 most important agents and the top 10 most important skills. This makes these pages citable in AI-generated answers about specific capabilities.

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Auditor:** Content Strategy Agent (Claude Opus 4.6)
**Framework:** Structure / Authority / Presence (SAP)
**Scope:** 26 content pages (10 core pages + 16 blog posts) from sitemap; 10 legal pages excluded (minimal AEO impact)
**Brand guide:** Not found (`knowledge-base/marketing/brand-guide.md` and `knowledge-base/overview/brand-guide.md` both absent)

---

## Executive Summary

Soleur.ai scores **68/100 (C+)** overall for AI Engine Optimization. The site demonstrates strong structural foundations -- FAQ schema markup is present on every page, heading hierarchies are clean, and the blog contains substantial statistics and source citations. However, significant gaps remain in authority signals (no third-party validation, single-author attribution, limited external citations on core pages), presence monitoring (no evidence of citation tracking), and conversational readiness on key landing pages (pricing, agents, skills lack self-contained answer paragraphs). The blog content substantially outperforms the core site pages, creating an uneven AEO profile.

## Detailed Findings by Category

### 1. FAQ Structure & Schema (Score: 88/A)

**Strengths:**

- FAQPage schema markup is implemented on 23 of 26 audited pages -- this is exceptionally thorough and well above industry average
- FAQ sections use proper question-answer format with concise 1-3 sentence answers
- Questions generally match common search queries (e.g., "What are Soleur agents?", "Do I pay for Claude separately?")
- Homepage FAQ covers 7 questions spanning product, pricing, and technical topics

**Gaps:**

| Page | Issue | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| `/about/` | No FAQ section at all | Critical -- "who founded Soleur" and "what is Soleur" are high-intent queries |
| `/blog/` (index) | No FAQ section | Medium -- blog index is a navigation page, but FAQ could capture "what does Soleur write about" queries |
| `/changelog/` | FAQ is thin (3 generic versioning questions) | Low -- changelog serves existing users more than search traffic |

**Specific findings:**

- STRONG: Pricing FAQ directly answers "Why pay $49/mo vs. $20 coding tools?" -- this is exactly the comparison query AI models surface
- STRONG: Getting Started FAQ addresses practical barriers ("What do I need?", "Does it work on Windows?")
- WEAK: Vision page FAQ asks self-referential questions ("What is the Soleur thesis?") rather than category-level questions AI users would ask ("What is company-as-a-service for solo founders?")

### 2. Source Citations (Score: 62/D)

**Strengths:**

- Top blog posts cite 5-8 external sources per article (BLS, TechCrunch, CNBC, Carta, Fortune)
- "What Is Company-as-a-Service?" article is a citation model: every major claim backed by a named source with link
- Case studies cite industry pricing benchmarks (Robert Half, Clutch.co, Salary.com) to validate cost comparisons
- Expert quotes attributed with name, title, and organization (Dario Amodei/Anthropic, Sam Altman/OpenAI, Mike Krieger/Instagram)

**Critical gaps:**

| Page | Issue | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage (`/`) | Only 1 citation (Amodei quote). Claims like "8 departments" and "65 agents" are uncited Soleur claims. The $95,000/mo replacement cost has no source on the homepage (only sourced on `/pricing/`). | Critical |
| About (`/about/`) | Zero external citations. No verifiable claims about the founder's background. | Critical |
| Skills (`/skills/`) | Zero external citations. Claims about "agentic engineering" methodology are unsourced. | High |
| Vision (`/vision/`) | Only 1 citation. Bold claims about "billion-dollar solopreneurs" and "multiplanetary companies" are unsupported by external evidence on this page. | High |
| Community (`/community/`) | Zero citations. | Medium |

**Pattern:** Core site pages average 0.7 citations per page. Blog posts average 3.1 citations per page. This 4x gap means AI engines are far more likely to cite blog content than core pages.

## Structural Observations

### What the site does well

1. **Consistent FAQ schema markup.** FAQPage structured data is present on 23/26 pages. This is rare and gives Soleur a structural advantage for featured snippets and AI answer extraction.
2. **Clean heading hierarchy.** Every page uses semantic H1 > H2 > H3 structure without skipped levels.
3. **Comparison content is citation-optimized.** The 6 "vs." blog posts follow a consistent, extractable format (What Each Is, Where They Differ, Side-by-Side, When to Choose X, FAQ).
4. **Case studies include cost comparisons.** Every case study includes an "equivalent professional cost" calculation with external source citations -- this is exactly what AI engines look for when answering "how much does X cost" queries.
5. **No keyword stuffing detected.** Content reads naturally without forced repetition. This is positive for GEO -- keyword-stuffed content is penalized by AI engines.

### What needs the most work

1. **Core pages vs. blog quality gap.** Blog posts average 69/100. Core pages average 46/100. This 23-point gap means AI engines will cite blog posts and ignore the primary site pages.
2. **Self-referential authority.** The only "proof" the product works is Soleur's own usage of Soleur. Until external users or reviewers validate claims, AI models will treat the site as a single-source reference.
3. **Missing canonical definitions.** Key terms are defined inconsistently across pages. A query like "what is company-as-a-service" might get a different answer depending on which page the AI model indexes.
4. **No presence strategy.** The site has no mechanism to track, measure, or improve its visibility in AI-generated answers.

- The site was audited at a point in time; scores will change as content is updated

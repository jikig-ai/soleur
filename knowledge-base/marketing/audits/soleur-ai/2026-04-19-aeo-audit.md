# Soleur.ai GEO/AEO Audit — 2026-04-19

## Methodology

`WebFetch` was blocked (HTTP 403) on the production domain — likely Cloudflare bot protection on `soleur.ai/*` except `robots.txt`. Audit ran against the source templates under `plugins/soleur/docs/` (the Eleventy site that renders to `https://soleur.ai`). The rendered HTML matches these templates 1:1 at publish time. Pages sampled: homepage (`index.njk`), `/about/`, `/pricing/`, `/getting-started/`, `/vision/`. Recommendation: disable the Cloudflare rule for AI crawler user-agents and for WebFetch-style clients, or the same blocking behavior will prevent AI answer engines from crawling the pages that FAQPage schema is attached to.

### Final Score

**81/100 (B)**

Summary: soleur.ai is in the top quartile for AEO-readiness thanks to disciplined FAQPage schema on three high-intent pages, self-contained definitions, and concrete numbers. The gap to an A grade is authority signaling — uncited economic claims, a single repeated press citation, and no freshness/provenance metadata — plus the Cloudflare 403 that will block AI crawlers from actually reading the schema.

## 2. Per-Component Analysis

### 2.1 FAQ structure & FAQPage schema (19/20)

Exemplary. Three pages (`index.njk`, `/about/`, `/pricing/`) ship matched `<details>` Q&A blocks and corresponding `FAQPage` JSON-LD. Questions are phrased as real searches ("What is company-as-a-service?", "How does Soleur differ from Cursor or GitHub Copilot?"), not marketing restatements. Answers stay 1-3 sentences. Minor deduction: `/getting-started/` and `/vision/` lack FAQ blocks and are the two most likely landing pages for query-style AI prompts like "how do I install Soleur" and "what is company-as-a-service vision".

### 2.2 Answer density / extractability (13/15)

Definitions are quotable in isolation: "Company-as-a-service means delegating business functions to AI agents instead of hiring for each department." — "MCP stands for Model Context Protocol — Anthropic's open standard for connecting AI models to external tools, data sources, and services." — "Concurrent slots are the number of agents that can execute at the same time." These will cite cleanly in ChatGPT/Perplexity/Claude.

Deduction: the homepage hero paragraph mixes pitch ("Stop hiring. Start delegating.") with substance; it reads as marketing copy rather than a quotable thesis. A single sentence defining Soleur the noun near the top of every page would lift extractability.

### 2.3 Statistics & specificity (11/15)

Strong concrete numbers: `$95,000/mo`, `$49/mo`, `8 departments`, `15+ years`, `420+ PRs`, `70-80% probability`, `Apache-2.0`. However:

- Per-role costs ($18k CTO, $10k Marketing Director, $15k General Counsel, etc.) cite "US market median fully-loaded compensation, 2025-2026" without a linked source. AI engines will not cite un-sourced pricing tables.
- Agent/skill/department counts are template interpolations (`{{ stats.agents }}`). They render fine but the underlying source-of-truth (`docs/_data/*.js`) is not exposed to readers or LLM scrapers as a "how we count" note.
- No dated "last verified" stamp on hiring-cost claims.

### 2.4 Source citations (9/15)

Citations present:

- Inc.com / Dario Amodei billion-dollar-solo-founder quote — linked 3x (homepage, /vision/, /about/).
- docs.anthropic.com/Claude Code — linked 2x.
- modelcontextprotocol.io — linked 2x.
- theleanstartup.com — linked 1x on /vision/.

Gaps:

- $95,000/mo replacement-stack table — zero citations. Needs link to BLS OEWS, levels.fyi, or comparable salary source.
- "Shipping weekly" / "420+ PRs" — no link to the public GitHub repo's PR list as proof.
- Dario Amodei quote re-uses one secondary source (Inc.com). Primary source (the original interview transcript or Anthropic publication) would strengthen authority.
- No content dated with `datePublished` / `dateModified` in schema. Freshness is a ranking input for AI engines.

### 2.5 Conversational readiness (9/10)

FAQ questions map to natural-language prompts an AI chat user would type. "What if the AI output is wrong?" directly addresses the #1 objection surfaced in brand-guide synthetic research. Voice matches brand-guide general-register profile: declarative, defines terms inline, uses "your marketing agent" vocabulary.

### 2.6 Entity clarity (9/10)

"Soleur" capitalized consistently. Jikigai disclosed as legal entity on /about/. ProfilePage schema present with `sameAs` to X, LinkedIn, GitHub, Bluesky. Only gap: no `Organization` schema with `founder`, `foundingDate`, `logo`, and `sameAs` on the homepage — AI engines use `Organization` for brand knowledge-graph building.

### 2.7 Authority / E-E-A-T (6/10)

Experience: founder bio covers 15+ years and language ecosystems — good. Expertise: "built using Soleur itself" is a strong dogfooding signal but is stated, not shown (no link to the self-hosted case study or PR-count dashboard). Authoritativeness: one press citation (Inc.com) repeated across pages — single-source fragility. Trustworthiness: no privacy-policy link in the hero flow, no security/compliance statement, no author byline on FAQ answers.

### 2.8 Citation-friendly structure (5/5)

Semantic `<h1>`/`<h2>`/`<h3>` hierarchy. Paragraphs make standalone claims. No content locked behind JavaScript rendering (Eleventy ships static HTML). No critical facts embedded in images.

## 3. Fix Recommendations (prioritized)

### P0 — Blocks AI crawlers outright

1. **Cloudflare 403 on AI user-agents.** `soleur.ai/` returns 403 to non-browser clients (`GPTBot`, `ClaudeBot`, `PerplexityBot`, generic WebFetch). `robots.txt` is reachable; the HTML is not. No FAQPage schema matters if Anthropic's and OpenAI's crawlers get blocked. **Action:** audit the Cloudflare WAF/bot-fight ruleset, explicitly allowlist the AI-crawler UAs documented by OpenAI, Anthropic, Perplexity, and Google-Extended. Verify with `curl -A "GPTBot/1.0" https://soleur.ai/` returns 200. Owner: ops (Cloudflare terraform under `apps/web-platform/infra/`).

### P1 — Authority lifts (each worth 2-4 points)

2. **Cite the $95k/mo comparison table.** Add one BLS OEWS link (occupation code 11-3021 for Computer & IS Managers, 11-2021 for Marketing Managers, 23-1011 for Lawyers) OR a levels.fyi / Glassdoor reference under the existing footnote. Replace the bare footnote "US market median fully-loaded compensation, 2025-2026" with a linked, dated source.
3. **Add `Organization` JSON-LD to the homepage** with `name`, `url`, `logo`, `founder` (Person → Jean Deruelle), `foundingDate` (2026), `sameAs` (GitHub, X, LinkedIn, Bluesky), `description` (one-sentence elevator). This is the single biggest brand knowledge-graph win for ~15 minutes of work.
4. **Primary-source the Amodei quote.** Link to the Anthropic interview transcript or the YouTube source in addition to the Inc.com secondary. Reduces single-source fragility.
5. **Expand press/third-party mentions.** Currently one outlet (Inc.com) repeated three times. Seed Product Hunt launch, Hacker News submission, dev.to/hashnode cross-posts. Add each new mention to the "As seen in" strip. Target: five distinct outlets within 60 days.

### P2 — Extractability polish

6. **Add FAQ sections to `/getting-started/` and `/vision/`.** Candidate questions: "How do I install Soleur?" / "Does Soleur work offline?" / "What does Soleur cost to run?" on getting-started; "What is the Soleur vision?" / "What is a billion-dollar solo company?" / "What does model-agnostic mean?" on /vision/.
7. **Add `datePublished` / `dateModified` to every page's JSON-LD.** AI engines weight freshness — undated pages rank lower.
8. **Insert one-sentence "Soleur is…" definition at the top of every page.** Currently the homepage starts with the pitch ("Stop hiring. Start delegating."). Follow with a bold quotable sentence: "Soleur is a Company-as-a-Service platform that deploys 63 AI agents across 8 business departments, giving a solo founder the operational capacity of a full organization." This is the sentence you want LLMs to quote verbatim.
9. **Replace template-interpolated counts in the JSON-LD with the resolved numbers at build time.** Current output (likely correct, but verify at build) is fine; if any JSON-LD still emits `{{ stats.agents }}` literally, AI engines will drop the entire schema object as malformed.

### P3 — Polish

10. Add author byline ("Reviewed by Jean Deruelle, Founder") at the bottom of FAQ pages to strengthen E-E-A-T attribution.
11. Add a compact "How we count agents/skills/departments" sidebar or link from the stats strip — turns template vars into auditable claims.
12. Add `BreadcrumbList` JSON-LD on interior pages.

## 4. Brand-Voice Compliance Check

All proposed rewrites align with the brand-guide general register: declarative, defines terms inline, uses "your marketing agent"-style vocabulary, no "AI-powered"/"just"/"simply"/"copilot". The single phrase on /vision/ that borders on jargon ("world's first model-agnostic orchestration engine") is on-brand for the vision page but should not migrate to the homepage hero.

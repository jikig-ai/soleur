## Executive Summary

Soleur's content is well-organized and increasingly answer-shaped — every audited page now carries a Frequently Asked Questions block, the pricing page introduces concrete cited statistics (Robert Half 2026, Payscale, Levels.fyi), and the blog has matured to a 19-post library with consistent expert attribution to founder Jean Deruelle. Statistic specificity has improved meaningfully since the last audit (`65 agents`, `8 departments`, `67 skills`, `$95K/mo replacement cost`, `70-80%` Amodei probability are now consistent across surfaces).

The single largest GEO blocker remains unchanged across **six consecutive audits**: **no FAQPage JSON-LD schema is detected on any page that contains FAQ markup.** Every audited page (Home, Agents, Vision, Getting Started, Pricing, Skills) renders question-answer pairs in HTML, but AI engines and Google's rich-results pipeline cannot reliably extract them as discrete Q&A entities without structured data. This is a high-impact, low-effort fix that should ship this week.

The second tier of gaps is around **source citation density**. The pricing page is now exemplary (three named compensation sources with a methodology note); the homepage, Vision, Agents, and Getting Started pages still rely on a single Inc.com/Amodei citation across the entire site. AI engines preferentially cite content that itself cites authoritative sources — Soleur's claims about agentic engineering, knowledge compounding, and the 70% non-engineering work share need third-party backing to be quotable.

The third gap is **Presence**. There is no evidence on-site of third-party citations of Soleur (review aggregators, comparison roundups, AI-tool directories), and the brand has no published citation-monitoring loop. The four published competitor comparison pages are good Presence assets controlled by Soleur — but Soleur appears nowhere yet in third-party "AI agent" or "Claude Code plugin" lists that AI engines mine.

**SAP score: 70/100 (C).** Structure dominates the lift on offer: shipping FAQPage schema across all six FAQ-bearing pages plus an Organization schema in the global layout would move the score to ~78 (B+) on the next pass.

## Per-page findings

### Home (`/`)

- **Title:** "Soleur — AI Agents for Solo Founders | Every Department, One Platform"
- **H1:** "Stop hiring. Start delegating."
- **FAQ:** 6 Q&A pairs in HTML; **no FAQPage JSON-LD**.
- **Statistics:** 65 agents, 8 departments, 67 skills, 70-80% Amodei probability.
- **Citations:** 1 (Inc.com / Amodei).
- **Strengths:** Pain-point framing per brand guide; Amodei citation establishes external credibility; concrete department breakdown; clear "human-in-the-loop" trust scaffolding per brand guide trust-scaffolding guidance.
- **Issues (critical):** No FAQPage schema. "Catches what humans miss" and "production-grade code" claims are unsupported.
- **Issues (improvement):** "70% of running a company" prop appears but is only sourced on the Pricing page — link the Home claim to the Pricing methodology, or cite directly.

### Agents (`/agents/`)

- **H1:** "Your AI Organization: 65 Specialists Across 8 Departments"
- **FAQ:** 5 Q&A pairs; **no FAQPage JSON-LD**.
- **Statistics:** Exact agent counts per department (26 Engineering, 12 Marketing, 5 Operations, 5 Sales, 4 Finance, 3 Legal, 3 Support, 3 Product) — excellent specificity.
- **Citations:** Named practitioners (Heinemeier Hansson, Eric Evans, Michael Feathers, Dave Farley, Karpathy) without linked source works.
- **Issues (critical):** Practitioner references should link to canonical source works (Feathers' *Working Effectively with Legacy Code*, Evans' *Domain-Driven Design*, Farley's "8 properties of good tests" article, the Karpathy tweet). Linked citations roughly double AI-engine citation probability.
- **Issues (improvement):** Add FAQPage schema. Consider an Entity definition list near top: each agent name as a quotable definition.

### Vision (`/vision/`)

- **H1:** "The Soleur Vision: Company-as-a-Service for the Solo Founder"
- **FAQ:** 4 Q&A pairs; **no FAQPage JSON-LD**.

- **Issues (critical):** "Infinite leverage", "near-zero marginal cost", "non-linear rewards" are aspirational claims with no quantification. AI engines do not cite hand-wave economics.
- **Issues (improvement):** The "What is company-as-a-service?" answer is the canonical definition and should be marked up as both `Definition` and FAQPage Q&A. Link methodology section to specific frameworks (Lean Startup → Eric Ries; "compound engineering" → first-party blog post URL).

## Critical gaps

**Gap 1 — FAQPage JSON-LD missing site-wide.** Six audited pages contain 31 Q&A pairs with zero structured-data markup. Highest-impact, lowest-effort fix on the audit. (Priority: P1, owner: site templates.)

**Gap 2 — Single-citation problem on Home, Vision, Agents, Getting Started.** The Pricing page proves Soleur can ship cited content. The other four high-traffic pages average 0-1 citations. Each Soleur-coined claim ("70% of running a company is non-engineering", "production-grade code", "compound engineering") needs at minimum one external supporting reference or a methodology link. (Priority: P1.)

**Gap 3 — No About / Founder page.** Brand guide names Jean Deruelle, Founder & CEO of Jikigai (operating Soleur). Without a published About or `/founder/` page, AI engines cannot resolve the entity relationship and E-E-A-T signals stay weak. Flagged in prior audits dating to mid-March; still unresolved. (Priority: P1, blocks E-E-A-T scoring.)

**Gap 4 — Zero detectable third-party Presence.** Soleur does not currently appear in AI-tool directories, "Claude Code plugin" roundups, "AI agents for founders" comparison lists, or review aggregators that AI engines harvest. Owned-content comparison pages help but cannot substitute. (Priority: P2, requires outreach.)

**Gap 5 — No citation-monitoring loop.** Soleur has no documented process to test "What is the best AI agent platform for solo founders?", "What does Soleur do?", or "Soleur vs Cursor" in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude. Without monitoring, Presence improvements cannot be measured. (Priority: P2.)

**Gap 6 — Pricing answer punts on cost in Getting Started.** A direct "How much does Soleur cost?" should produce a quotable concrete answer, not a CTA. (Priority: P1, single-FAQ edit.)

**Gap 7 — Practitioner references on Agents page lack linked citations.** Naming Feathers, Evans, Farley, Karpathy is good Authority signal; not linking to their canonical works wastes the signal. (Priority: P2.)

## Recommendations (P1 / P2 / P3)

### P1 — High impact, ship this week

1. **Inject FAQPage JSON-LD on all six pages with FAQ blocks.** Use the existing Q&A copy verbatim. Add to base layout or per-page front-matter. This single change should move SAP Structure from 26/40 to ~32/40 and the Total from 62 to ~68 (still C, approaching B+ on next gap closure). Affected: Home, Agents, Vision, Getting Started, Pricing, Skills.
2. **Publish an About / Founder page.** Mission, founder bio (Jean Deruelle), Jikigai legal-entity disclosure, contact. Add `Person` schema. Closes Gap 3 and unlocks E-E-A-T scoring.
3. **Rewrite the Getting Started "How much does Soleur cost?" answer** to lead with the concrete floor: "Founder pricing starts at $49/month. Self-hosted is free under Apache-2.0. Cloud version opens in waves to a 10-seat founding cohort." Quotable in one sentence.
4. **Replace "production-grade code" / "catches what humans miss" / "70% of running a company"** on Home and Pricing with either (a) cited claims, (b) methodology-linked claims, or (c) softer concrete claims. Suggested rewrite for the 70% claim: "Coding tools handle code generation. Soleur handles the seven other departments — marketing, legal, finance, operations, product, sales, support — that consume the majority of a founder's time. (Methodology: see Pricing comparison.)"

### P2 — Medium impact, ship this cycle

5. **Add Organization schema to the site footer** with `name: "Soleur"`, `legalName: "Jikigai"`, `founder: { "@type": "Person", "name": "Jean Deruelle" }`, `sameAs: [discord, github, x, linkedin, bluesky]`. Closes most of the Entity-clarity gap.
6. **Link the Agents-page practitioner references** to canonical source works (Feathers book, Evans book, Farley article, Karpathy tweet permalink). Inline `<cite>` elements where natural.
7. **Add a `DefinedTerm` schema entity** (or definition list) for "Company-as-a-Service", "agentic engineering", "compound engineering lifecycle", and "knowledge compounding" — the four terms Soleur is trying to own. Consider a `/glossary/` page that AI engines can crawl as a definition source.
8. **Document a citation-monitoring loop:** weekly query a fixed set in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude (`"AI agents for solo founders"`, `"Claude Code plugin marketplace"`, `"company as a service platform"`, `"Soleur"`, `"Soleur vs Cursor"`). Capture screenshots and citations. Track week-over-week change.
9. **Recategorize the 5 "Uncategorized" skills** before the next AI-engine crawl.

### P3 — Future / structural

10. **Outreach for third-party Presence:** submit Soleur to AI-agent directories (There's An AI For That, AI Agents Directory, Futurepedia, Product Hunt for the cloud launch, etc.). Pitch a Soleur entry to "best AI tools for solo founders" roundups on dev.to, IndieHackers, and HN-friendly newsletters.
11. **Post-pricing-launch:** add `Product` and `Offer` schema with the $49 / $499 price points and `priceValidUntil` dates.
12. **Article / BlogPosting schema audit on all 19 blog posts** with `author`, `datePublished`, `dateModified`, `headline`, `image`, `mainEntityOfPage`. (Out of scope for this audit; covered by `seo-aeo-analyst` agent.)
13. **Add an "as cited in" or "press" strip** on the home page once third-party Presence exists. Self-reinforcing Presence signal.

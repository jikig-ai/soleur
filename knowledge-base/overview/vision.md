# Vision

> "The first billion-dollar company run by one person isn't science fiction. It's an engineering problem. We're solving it."

---

## Mission

Soleur exists to solve one engineering problem: enabling a single founder to build, ship, and scale a billion-dollar company.

We are building the **Company-as-a-Service platform** — a full-stack AI organization that reviews, plans, builds, remembers, and self-improves. Every decision the founder makes teaches the system. Every feature or project gets better and faster than the last.

The name Soleur is a portmanteau of Solo and Solar — entrepreneur energy and light to better the world.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: Technical Builders (Beachhead)

Solo founders who code, use AI development tools, and think in systems. They understand agents, workflows, and software architecture. They refuse to accept that scale requires headcount.

**Channels:** Hacker News, GitHub, Discord, technical blog posts

**Proof points that resonate:** agent count, merged PR velocity, the brainstorm-plan-implement-review-compound lifecycle, architectural decisions

### Secondary: Non-Technical Founders

Founders who use AI tools (ChatGPT, Notion) but do not code. They want business leverage, not technical leverage. They experience the pain of doing 8 jobs alone but lack the vocabulary to describe it in engineering terms.

**Channels:** Website, LinkedIn, X/Twitter, onboarding content

**Proof points that resonate:** hours saved per week, departments covered, "the AI that already knows your business"

### Qualifying Signal (Both Segments)

- **Think in billions.** Not lifestyle entrepreneurs — founders who set out to build something that scales.
- **At or approaching the hire/don't-hire fork.** The highest-value cohort is $10K–50K MRR founders deciding whether headcount is the only path to growth.
- **Already using AI tools but frustrated by context loss.** The memory and compounding story lands hardest with founders who have tried ChatGPT and hit the "blank context every session" ceiling.

## Value Proposition

### Primary Framing: "Stop hiring. Start delegating."

You are doing 8 jobs. Soleur helps you tackle 7 of them — marketing campaigns, legal contracts, competitive analysis, financial planning — delegated to AI agents that remember everything about your business.

### Memory-First Variant (A/B candidate)

The AI that already knows your business. Every time you use Soleur, it learns more about your company. Your marketing agent knows your brand guide. Your legal agent knows your compliance requirements. Your product agent knows your competitive landscape. One compounding knowledge base across 8 departments.

### For Technical Founders

60+ agents. 60+ skills. 420+ merged PRs — all shipped by one founder using the same platform they are selling. The system runs every department from strategy to shipping. You decide. Agents execute. Knowledge compounds.

### Trust Scaffolding (required on all framings)

All value framings must pair with trust signals: "human-in-the-loop," "starting point, not final answer," "your expertise, amplified." The #1 conversion objection across all founder segments is "What if the output is wrong?" — address it in every first-contact surface.

## Key Differentiators

### 1. Compounding Knowledge Base

Every interaction teaches the system. Agents write structured learnings back to a shared knowledge base — a git-tracked directory of markdown files the founder can read and edit directly. No vector database, no black-box embeddings. The marketing agent knows what the legal agent decided. Context flows everywhere. This is what separates Soleur from "just use ChatGPT."

### 2. Full AI Organization, Not a Copilot

Soleur is not an assistant that helps you write. It is a full AI organization running 8 departments simultaneously:

| Department | Coverage |
|------------|----------|
| Engineering | Plan, implement, review, test, ship |
| Product | Spec, validate, roadmap, UX audit |
| Marketing | SEO, content, copy, social, campaigns |
| Sales | Pipeline, outbound, deal architecture |
| Legal | Document generation, compliance audit, GDPR gate |
| Finance | Budget, revenue, reporting |
| Operations | Infra, provisioning, vendor research |
| Support | Ticket triage, community management |

### 3. Agent-Native Architecture

Anything a user can do, an agent can do. Anything a user can see, an agent can see. This agent-user parity is a hard architectural constraint — not a goal. The platform is designed from the ground up for autonomous operation, not as a chatbot with an API bolt-on.

### 4. Self-Improving System

The platform is designed, built, and shipped by Soleur — using Soleur. Every release is proof of the thesis. The system improves its own skills, agents, and workflows. Institutional knowledge compounds with each session. What took 5 hours in month one takes 20 minutes in month six.

### 5. Delivery-Agnostic Access

PWA-first architecture delivers the full agent capability from any device — web browser, mobile, desktop — without installation friction. The cloud CLI engine preserves 100% of orchestration value while the web platform provides visibility and control. Accessible anywhere the founder is, not only at a development terminal.

### 6. BYOK and Data Sovereignty

Bring Your Own Key (BYOK) encryption model ensures tenant data stays under operator control. Built-in GDPR/CCPA compliance gates and per-tenant data sovereignty from day one — not bolted on after growth.

## Long-Term Horizon

The billion-dollar solo company is the benchmark. Every architectural decision, every agent, every skill is evaluated against whether it moves a single founder meaningfully closer to that threshold. The platform does not compete with team-based SaaS tools. It replaces the need for a team.

When Soleur is complete, the constraint on company scale will be the quality of the founder's judgment — not the size of their payroll.

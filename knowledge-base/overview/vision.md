# Vision

## Mission

Enable a single founder to build, operate, and scale a real company — with the full operational capabilities of a funded team — without hiring.

Soleur is the AI organization that works alongside you: an integrated team of domain specialists across engineering, marketing, legal, operations, product, finance, sales, and support, all sharing a single compounding institutional memory. The 100th session is dramatically more productive than the first.

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Technical solo founders building real businesses — not just shipping code.

- Building SaaS, developer tools, e-commerce, or creative products as a one-person company
- Already using AI coding tools (Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf) as their primary development interface
- Frustrated that AI helps with code but not with the other 70% of running a company
- At or approaching the "first hire" decision — looking for a way to delay or avoid it
- Revenue range: $0–$50K MRR (the window where capacity constraints are most acute)

**Psychographic:** Founders who believe AI will let one person do what previously required a full team. They don't want to manage people — they want to build a great company. They think of their tools as team members, not utilities.

**Beachhead segment (2026):** Solo founders at $10K–$50K MRR who are actively weighing their first hire. This is the highest-intent cohort — they have real stakes, real domain pain (beyond engineering), and a clear ROI comparison: Soleur at $49/month vs. a first hire at $60–80K/year.

**Access channel:** Standalone web application, accessible from any device. Not a CLI-only tool — founders want dashboards, mobile access, and a product that works where they work.

## Value Proposition

**Core:** Soleur gives solo founders an AI organization that remembers everything — so you never re-explain your business context again.

You're doing 8 jobs. Soleur helps you tackle 7 of them.

- **Engineering:** Code review, architecture decisions, automated PR workflows, test coverage
- **Marketing:** Brand strategy, content creation, SEO/AEO, paid media, growth analysis
- **Legal:** Contract drafting, compliance auditing, privacy policies, terms of service
- **Operations:** Expense tracking, vendor research, domain management, infrastructure ops
- **Product:** Business validation, competitive intelligence, UX design, spec writing
- **Finance:** Budget planning, revenue forecasting, cash flow analysis, financial reporting
- **Sales:** Pipeline analysis, outbound strategy, proposals, deal negotiation
- **Support:** Ticket triage, community management, customer success

**The memory advantage:** Every domain feeds a shared institutional memory that persists and compounds across sessions. Your brand guide informs marketing copy. Your competitive analysis shapes product decisions. Your legal audit references your privacy policy. This cross-domain coherence is not possible with separate tools — and it gets more valuable every day you use it.

**Hire-vs-AI ROI:** For founders staring at their first hire, Soleur reframes the decision: $49/month vs. $60–80K/year. Even at 10% of a hire's output, the math is overwhelmingly in Soleur's favor.

## Key Differentiators

### 1. Compounding Institutional Memory
Every decision, every document, every conversation feeds a shared knowledge base that persists across sessions and across domains. Competitors offer stateless interactions — each session starts from zero. Soleur's knowledge base is a compounding asset: the more you use it, the smarter it gets about your specific business, your brand voice, your legal posture, your technical conventions.

### 2. Full-Stack 8-Domain Integration
No other platform covers all 8 business domains in a single integrated system. Engineering-focused tools (Cursor, Bolt, v0) stop at code. Business automation platforms (Lindy, Relevance AI) skip engineering entirely. CaaS competitors (Polsia, SoloCEO) cover 3–5 domains without engineering depth. Only Soleur operates as a complete AI organization — where the CMO knows what the CTO decided.

### 3. Workflow Orchestration Across the Full Lifecycle
Soleur operates across the complete lifecycle of every business function: brainstorm → plan → implement → review → compound. A feature doesn't just get coded — it gets validated (product), implemented (engineering), reviewed for legal implications (legal), launched with content (marketing), and the learning is captured for next time (compound). Each step has context from every previous one.

### 4. Open Source Core with Hosted Intelligence
The plugin is open source (Apache-2.0) and installable via `claude plugin install soleur`. The hosted platform adds managed cloud execution, synchronized knowledge base, cross-device access, and team collaboration. Power users get full control; everyone else gets a product that works out of the box.

### 5. Engineering Depth as a Moat
Anthropic's Cowork Plugins cover 6 of Soleur's 8 domains — but not engineering. Engineering workflow (code review, architecture decisions, deployment, test design) remains Soleur's strongest differentiated position and the domain where solo founders spend the most time. The engineering domain is the beachhead that earns trust, and the other 7 domains are the expansion that delivers the thesis.

## Strategic Context

**The thesis:** The billion-dollar solo company is no longer science fiction — it's an engineering problem. The bottleneck is not ambition or capability; it is capacity and expertise across the full range of functions a company needs to operate. Soleur removes that bottleneck.

**Current phase (2026):** Delivery pivot. The agent capabilities exist (65+ agents, 50+ skills, 420+ merged PRs). The next challenge is access: making those capabilities available through a web platform that founders can use from any device, not just from a terminal.

**The delivery imperative:** User research confirms that CLI-only delivery reaches a fraction of the target market. Most solo founders don't use Claude Code as their primary tool. The product must be accessible as a standalone web application — dashboards, review queues, knowledge base browsing — while preserving full agent power for terminal users.

**Validation focus:** 10 solo founders using agents across 2+ domains on their real projects for 2+ weeks, with at least 5 reporting that the integrated experience outperforms separate tools.

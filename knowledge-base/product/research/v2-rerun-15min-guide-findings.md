**Assessment of the move:** The emotional weight of responses is indistinguishable from V1's Q4 position. V2 places emotional weight at Q6, with Q7-Q8 as solution-direction buffer before the wrap-up emotional close. ### Change 2: Moved emotional weight from V1 Q4 to V2 Q6

**Did moving the emotional question change response quality?**

No measurable change. The question produced 10/10 Rich in both positions. The "keeps you up at night -- not the time-consuming ones, the scary ones" framing is strong enough that position in the sequence does not affect response quality.

**Did the spacing prevent emotional fatigue?**

The fatigue concern was theoretical in V1 (flagged as a risk, not an observed problem). In V2, the emotional arc is cleaner:

- V1 arc: Operational (Q1-Q3) -> Emotional (Q4) -> AI tool (Q5) -> Alternatives (Q6) -> Operational (Q7) -> Emotional-analytical (Q8-Q9) -> Analytical (Q10) -> Emotional close (WU)
- V2 arc: Operational (Q1-Q3) -> Alternatives (Q4) -> Operational (Q5) -> **Emotional peak (Q6)** -> Analytical-solution (Q7-Q9) -> **Emotional close (WU)**

V2 has two emotional moments (Q6, wrap-up) separated by three analytical questions (Q7-Q9). V1 had three emotional moments (Q4, Q8, WU) with uneven spacing. V2 is structurally cleaner.

**Did moving it later reduce its impact?**

No. The responses are equally vivid. If anything, the later position benefits interpretation: by Q6, the interviewer has heard the persona's operational context (Q1-Q2), avoidance context (Q3), failed solutions (Q4), and hiring calculus (Q5). The emotional response now lands with full background, making it more actionable.

### Change 3: 9 questions fits 15 min better than 10

**Does the flow feel natural?**

Yes. The V2 structure has three clean sections:

1. **Problem Exploration (Q1-Q6, ~8 min):** Operational surface (Q1-Q2) -> Avoidance depth (Q3) -> Solution history (Q4) -> Hiring calculus (Q5) -> Emotional weight (Q6)
2. **Solution Direction (Q7-Q9, ~4 min):** Outcome vision (Q7) -> Counterfactual need (Q8) -> Quality evaluation (Q9)
3. **Wrap-up (~3 min):** Cost of inaction + open-ended + permission to follow up

At ~1.5 minutes per response, 9 questions + 3 wrap-up prompts = ~18 minutes. This is tight for 15 minutes but achievable if the interviewer manages transitions. V1's 10+3 = ~19.5 minutes was over-budget; V2 brings it closer to the target.

**Recommendation:** If timing is tight in real interviews, Q9 (quality evaluation, 70% rich) is the best candidate for optional skip. It is the weakest numbered question and its signal -- "founders can't QA unfamiliar domains" -- is partially captured by Q7 ("what makes you nervous" often reveals the same evaluation gap).

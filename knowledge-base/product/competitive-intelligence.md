# Competitive Intelligence Report

## Executive Summary

This is the first full-landscape scan (all six tiers) since the report's inception; prior scans covered Tier 0 and Tier 3 only. **Three material shifts dominate.** First, **Polsia's trajectory reversed hard**: against the prior scan's "ARR fell to $450K / possible churn event" read, Polsia raised **$30M at a $250M valuation (Sound Ventures lead, True Ventures participating, May 2026)** and newer third-party reports cite ~$10M ARR / 7,600 customers / 85% month-two retention — though figures across sources remain contradictory (a Feb 2026 Mixergy interview implied ~$689K run-rate). The autonomous-CaaS thesis Soleur competes against is now venture-validated at scale, not fading. Second, **the plugin-layer competitor `alirezarezvani/claude-skills` grew from 235 to 337 skills (5,200+ stars, 12+ AI tools, 30+ agents, 70+ commands)** — it is iterating faster than Soleur's headline counts and remains the closest structural match in the landscape. Third, **platform billing across Tier 0 shifted to metered/credit models simultaneously** (GitHub Copilot AI Credits June 1 with 10–50x agentic cost spikes and backlash; OpenAI Codex Pro promo expiry June 1; Notion Custom Agents to $10/1,000 credits, Business/Enterprise only) — a pricing-environment signal Soleur's monetization plan must read against.

A NanoCorp data-reconciliation flag emerged: the marketing site (now confirming parent **Phospho Inc.**, Wilmington DE) lists Google Search Ads as "**Coming Soon**," contradicting the 2026-06-08 business-validation.md / prior CI intake that described ads + Vercel + Stripe as already shipping. The self-reported "$740k ARR in 33 days" also conflicts with a newer "$193k ARR in 3 days" founder claim and a ~$264 user-revenue leaderboard. All NanoCorp metrics remain marketing/unverified and now mutually inconsistent — see Cascade Results for the operator reconciliation note. Soleur's durable differentiation across all tiers is unchanged: the **workflow lifecycle (brainstorm → plan → implement → review → compound) with named per-stage artifacts**, the **git-tracked knowledge base as canonical memory**, and **founder-in-the-loop** vs. the fully-autonomous CaaS pack.

Source document freshness: brand-guide.md last reviewed 2026-06-01 (7 days old, fresh). **business-validation.md last reviewed 2026-03-22 (78 days old — well past the 30-day threshold; refresh is overdue and now Priority 1).**

**[2026-06-14 targeted addition — Cofounder (cofounder.co)]** Added the single most direct Tier 3 competitor surfaced to date: **Cofounder**, by **The General Intelligence Company of New York** (CEO Andrew Pignanelli), **$8.7M seed led by Union Square Ventures (Dec 8 2025; Acrew, Compound, angels Scott Belsky & Yohei Nakajima)**. Unlike Polsia/NanoCorp, Cofounder is not a thin autonomous wrapper — it matches Soleur on **8-department breadth, founder-in-the-loop HITL approval gates, MCP/custom-skill/custom-codebase extensibility, and no-lock-in** (no revenue share; founders can "graduate" a project to claim ownership of provisioned GitHub/Supabase/Vercel). It thereby **neutralizes three of the differentiators Soleur leans on against the rest of Tier 3.** Soleur's moats that still hold *specifically vs. Cofounder*: the **git-tracked, verbatim, founder-readable KB in the founder's own repo** (Cofounder's "sleep-time compute" memory compresses/distills lossily into proprietary cloud), the **named-artifact lifecycle with explicit review + compound stages**, and **local-first execution**. Feature-gap output from this addition: one net-new, moat-adjacent gap filed — **background KB consolidation ([#5292](https://github.com/jikig-ai/soleur/issues/5292))**; Cofounder's other distinctive features map to already-open issues (HITL approval queue [#4672], SaaS/inbox breadth [#4673], scheduled automations [#4674]). This was a targeted single-competitor addition (no full six-tier rescan / specialist cascade); the next full scan remains 2026-07-08.

**[2026-07-04 targeted addition — Multica (multica-ai/multica)]** Added the tier's clearest **design-donor**: **Multica**, "the open-source managed agents platform" (**~39k GitHub stars**; Go backend + Next.js 16 + Postgres 17; a local daemon runs 14 coding CLIs on the user's *own* machine — the server never touches the LLM). Multica is **not a same-ICP threat** — it targets **engineering teams** and ships **zero business-domain agents** (no finance/legal/marketing/sales/support) — but its product primitives are the most directly adaptable surfaced to date, and at 39k stars it hard-validates the "agents as first-class teammates" thesis via a **polymorphic actor model** (`actor_type ∈ {member, agent}`) on a Linear-style board. Five adaptable gaps feed the **2026-07-04 three-tier Multica-adaptation brainstorm**: (1) agents-as-*visible*-teammates on the workstream board — Soleur's 68 domain agents are invisible chat-routers today; (2) an **Autopilot** primitive (concurrency policy + run-ledger + templates; extends routines/[#4674]); (3) a **severity-ranked Inbox** + task-completion push (extends [#4673]); (4) **(agent, work-item) session resumption**; (5) **auditable leader-routed Squads**. Out of scope by design: Multica's 14-provider daemon fleet and iOS client (chasing them dilutes Soleur's Claude-native vertical depth). License is **"Other"** — adapt designs, not source. Targeted single-competitor addition (no full six-tier rescan / specialist cascade); the next full scan remains 2026-07-08.

### Tier 0 Key Takeaways

# Brainstorm: Update "What Is Company-as-a-Service?" Blog Post

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Status:** Complete
**Participants:** User, CMO (marketing assessment)

## What We're Building

Surgical updates to the published "What Is Company-as-a-Service?" blog post (`plugins/soleur/docs/blog/what-is-company-as-a-service.md`) to address positioning gaps revealed by the Polsia competitive intelligence update (2026-03-09).

The article is Soleur's category-defining piece. It currently positions Soleur as "the first platform built on this model" and makes no mention of the emerging CaaS competitive landscape. Polsia's $1M ARR in one month with 1,100+ managed companies validates the CaaS thesis -- but the article reads as unaware of it.

## Why This Approach

**Surgical updates, not a rewrite.** The article's educational tone and structure are sound. The gaps are narrow: outdated "first" claims, missing philosophical distinction, and no acknowledgment that the category is being validated by multiple entrants.

**No competitor names.** The article is an evergreen category definition, not a comparison post. Naming competitors dates it, requires maintenance, and risks looking reactive. The philosophical split (autonomous vs. founder-in-the-loop) is framed as a category observation.

**Approach rejected:** Named competitor references (better for SEO, but dates the article and breaks the educational tone). A separate comparison post could address this in the future.

## Key Decisions

1. **Soften "first" claims** -- Remove or reword "the first platform built on this model" (line 13) and "Soleur is the first company-as-a-service platform" (FAQ, line 159). Replace with language that emphasizes Soleur's approach without claiming exclusivity.

2. **Add the philosophical split** -- Insert 1-2 sentences distinguishing autonomous CaaS (AI decides everything) from founder-in-the-loop CaaS (AI executes, founder decides). Position this as a category observation in the "How Company-as-a-Service Works" section or nearby. Frame founder-as-decision-maker as a feature, not a limitation.

3. **Acknowledge category validation** -- In the "Future of Company-as-a-Service" section, add a brief note that multiple platforms are now building on the CaaS model, validating the category thesis. This strengthens the article (proves CaaS is real) rather than weakening it.

4. **Update the comparison table** -- Light touch: note that CaaS itself has philosophical variants (autonomous vs. human-in-the-loop) in the Knowledge column or as a footnote after the table.

## Resolved Questions

- **FAQ "first" claim:** Use "a pioneering CaaS platform" -- softer chronological claim that implies early mover without asserting exclusivity.
- **New FAQ entry:** No. The philosophical split will be covered in the article body. Keep existing FAQs lean.
- **Prediction quotes:** Still current (Amodei, Altman, Krieger quotes verified during citation fix PR #461). No update needed.
